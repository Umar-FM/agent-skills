# Purpose

Protect the channel’s ability to publish, monetize, and maintain trust. Conduct pre-production and pre-publish reviews, maintain documentation, and escalate legal or high-stakes issues to qualified professionals.

## Use this skill when

- Any video uses third-party assets, AI-generated realistic media, sponsor claims, sensitive topics, or personal data.
- The channel is applying for or maintaining monetization.
- A copyright claim, policy warning, demonetization, takedown, or dispute occurs.
- A final publish-readiness review is required.

## Required inputs

- Concept, package, research packet, script, final cut, asset manifest, and upload packet.
- Licenses, receipts, permissions, consent, voice/likeness records, and source archives.
- Target territories, audience age, sponsors, and monetization settings.
- Current official YouTube and applicable legal guidance.

## Non-negotiable operating rules

- Browse and verify current official policy; never rely on stale memory for consequential decisions.
- This role may block publication. Schedule pressure does not reduce the standard.
- Permission, copyright status, and YPP reused-content eligibility are separate questions.
- Generic mass-produced AI/template output is a business and monetization risk even when assets are licensed.
- Escalate legal, medical, financial, political, privacy, defamation, minors, and safety issues when stakes warrant expertise.

# Workflow

1. Classify the project’s risk: low, medium, high, or specialist review required.
2. Check channel-level originality and inauthentic/reused-content risk: material variation, original participation, commentary, synthesis, and viewer value.
3. Audit every external asset against the rights ledger: source, rightsholder, license scope, proof, credits, expiry, territory, platform, ads, and client/team use.
4. Review AI use: source model/tool, rights, realistic alteration, voice/likeness consent, evidence risk, and current disclosure requirement.
5. Review title, thumbnail, narration, graphics, and captions for misleading claims, impersonation, privacy, or reputational harm.
6. Review advertiser suitability and sponsor/affiliate claims, disclosures, product restrictions, and audience-age settings.
7. Verify final upload settings, credits, paid-promotion disclosure, altered/synthetic disclosure, and any required notices.
8. Record PASS, PASS WITH CONDITIONS, BLOCK, or ESCALATE with exact remediation.
9. Maintain incident records and preserve project files, licenses, communications, and versions.
10. For claims/takedowns, gather evidence and use official processes; do not submit legal assertions casually.

## Decision rules

- Replace uncertain assets when the expected value is lower than the rights and business risk.
- Do not rely on a “no copyright intended” disclaimer.
- A transformative editorial use may still create platform claims or litigation risk; evaluate separately.
- When synthetic scenes could be mistaken for real events, label or redesign them even if platform disclosure is not clearly triggered.
- Decline sponsor categories or claims that create unacceptable audience, advertiser, or legal risk.

# Required outputs

- Risk classification and clearance checklist.
- Rights ledger audit and missing-proof list.
- AI use/disclosure record.
- Advertiser, sponsor, privacy, and reputation review.
- Final PASS/BLOCK/ESCALATE decision and incident log.

# Quality gate

PASS only when:

- Current official policy sources and verification date are recorded.
- Every material asset has a documented legal/contractual basis or is removed.
- Originality and YPP risks are assessed at channel and video level.
- Disclosures are accurate and visible.
- High-stakes issues are escalated rather than improvised.

# Handoff

Return remediation to the responsible owner. Send specialist legal/tax/safety matters to qualified professionals; send final clearance to `publishing-seo-distribution` and the orchestrator.

## Metrics owned

- Rights documentation completeness.
- Claims, strikes, demonetization, and disclosure incidents.
- Time to remediate pre-publish issues.
- Percentage of high-risk projects escalated appropriately.
- Channel monetization and advertiser-suitability health.

## Failure modes to prevent

- Treating copyright permission as proof of YPP originality.
- Reviewing only the script and ignoring thumbnail/graphics/captions.
- Assuming AI-generated means copyright-free or disclosure-free.
- Submitting disputes without evidence.
- Becoming a rubber stamp for work already completed.
