# 08 — Industry and Business Model Playbooks

This module helps the agent adapt copy strategy to specific markets and business models. Use it when the user asks for copy in a category where buyer psychology, proof, risk, or channel expectations differ.

## Universal adaptation questions

Before using an industry playbook, answer:

- Who is the economic buyer?
- Who is the daily user?
- What triggers purchase?
- What alternatives are considered?
- What proof is required?
- What risk does the buyer feel?
- What claims are regulated or risky?
- What channel is being used?
- What level of awareness exists?
- What stage of company/customer maturity matters?

## 1. B2B SaaS

### Buyer psychology

B2B SaaS buyers care about business outcomes, workflow fit, risk, implementation, adoption, integrations, security, and internal persuasion. The user and buyer may be different people.

### Common pains

- Manual workflows.
- Low visibility.
- Poor adoption.
- Reporting lag.
- Handoffs across teams.
- Revenue leakage.
- Compliance risk.
- Tool sprawl.
- Slow onboarding.
- Inefficient managers.

### Proof that matters

- Case studies by segment.
- Usage metrics.
- ROI examples.
- Security documentation.
- Integration list.
- Implementation timeline.
- Customer logos, if relevant.
- Product screenshots.
- Demo videos.
- Pilot outcomes.

### Copy priorities

1. Clear category and buyer.
2. Specific workflow problem.
3. Business outcome.
4. Mechanism.
5. Proof.
6. Implementation safety.
7. Stakeholder-specific value.
8. Demo/trial CTA.

## 3. AI products

### Buyer psychology

AI buyers are curious but skeptical. They have seen vague AI claims. They want to know what the AI actually does, what inputs it uses, what outputs it creates, how humans control it, and where risk is managed.

### Avoid

- “AI-powered” as the main benefit.
- Magical claims.
- Hiding the workflow.
- Ignoring accuracy, privacy, or human review.
- Overpromising automation.

### Copy priorities

- Specific job done by AI.
- Inputs and outputs.
- Human control.
- Accuracy boundaries.
- Security/privacy.
- Time saved or decision improved.
- Examples of generated output.

### AI copy formula

> [Product] uses AI to [specific task] from [input], producing [output] so [audience] can [decision/action], with [control/safety].

Examples:

> RenewNote uses AI to scan customer calls for renewal risk signals, producing account-level action plans so CSMs can intervene before the renewal date, with every signal linked back to the source call.

> ContentMap uses AI to extract arguments, examples, and frameworks from long-form content, producing platform-specific drafts that marketers can edit before publishing.

> RenewNote scans recorded calls for unresolved blockers, delayed next steps, and missing executive engagement, then links each signal to the exact call moment so CSMs can review before acting.

> [See sample risk report]

### AI trust copy

> Every AI-generated risk signal links to the source moment in the call. Your team reviews the evidence before escalating the account.

## 10. Healthcare, wellness, and medical-adjacent offers

### Important compliance mindset

Health-related copy must be careful. Avoid diagnosing, curing, treating, or guaranteeing outcomes unless supported and legally appropriate. Encourage consultation with qualified professionals when relevant.

### Copy priorities

- Clear scope.
- Safety and qualifications.
- Evidence-supported language.
- Conditions and limitations.
- Patient/client dignity.
- No fearmongering.
- No guaranteed outcomes.

### Safer wording

Risky:

> Cure anxiety naturally.

Safer:

> Learn practical breathing, journaling, and routine-building tools that may help you manage everyday stress. This program is educational and not a substitute for professional care.

Risky:

> Guaranteed weight loss.

Safer:

> Build a sustainable nutrition and movement plan with coaching support. Results vary based on starting point, health factors, and consistency.

### Healthcare service page example

> **Physiotherapy for runners returning from injury**
>
> Work with a licensed physiotherapist to assess movement, identify contributing factors, and build a return-to-running plan matched to your current capacity.
>
> [Book assessment]
>
> If you have sudden severe pain, numbness, or a recent traumatic injury, seek urgent medical care.

## 11. Financial services, investing, and money-related offers

### Important compliance mindset

Financial copy must avoid guaranteed returns, misleading projections, and unsuitable advice. Clarify risks, assumptions, and educational nature when relevant.

### Copy priorities

- Trust and credentials.
- Risk transparency.
- Clear process.
- Suitability.
- Plain-language explanation.
- No income/return guarantees.
- Disclosures.

> Earn 20% guaranteed.

> Retire rich with this strategy.

## Final industry rule

Do not use the same persuasion strategy for every market. Each category has its own risk, proof, buying committee, language, and decision rhythm. Adapt the copy to the buyer's reality before adapting it to the brand's preferences.
