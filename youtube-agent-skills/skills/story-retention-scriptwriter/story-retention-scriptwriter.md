# Purpose

Convert a compelling concept into a viewing experience that continuously earns the next moment. Protect clarity and trust while using story, progress, stakes, surprise, proof, and emotion to maximize qualified AVD.

## Use this skill when

- Packaging and research are ready for scripting.
- Intro retention or mid-video AVD is weak.
- An essay, article, or research packet must become video-native.
- A faceless format needs recurring story architecture.
- The request is not primarily a sleep, bedtime-story, or low-arousal background-listening script; route those to `sleep-scriptwriter`.

## Required inputs

- Locked packaging brief and opening obligations.
- Research packet and visual proof opportunities.
- Target viewer, surface, runtime, and brand voice.
- Sponsor/CTA requirements and policy constraints.
- Retention postmortems from comparable videos.

## Non-negotiable operating rules

- The script serves the package; it does not begin with unrelated background.
- The first five seconds confirm the click with subject and proof.
- Use causal “but/therefore” progression instead of chronological “and then.”
- Open loops only when the eventual closure provides proportionate value.
- Every segment must change knowledge, progress, stakes, emotion, or perspective.
- Train the voice and constraints before drafting long-form scripts; do not rely on one all-purpose prompt.

# Workflow

1. Write the viewing contract: what the viewer expects from title/thumbnail, what proof confirms it, and what final payoff completes it.
2. Draft the 0-5 second click confirmation using the strongest available visual or result, without a logo, greeting, or biography.
3. Draft 5-15 seconds to establish consequence, stakes, conflict, or the central unanswered question.
4. Draft 15-30 seconds to show the mechanism/roadmap and deliver the first useful proof or micro-payoff.
5. Build a beat map in `shared/templates/retention_map.md`. For each beat, state the viewer question, new value, visual proof, emotion, and loop status.
6. Choose a story format such as origin, rise/fall, chain reaction, hidden truth, real reason, unseen system, or buried detail. Order beats by causal necessity and escalating interest.
7. Create progress devices: tests, thresholds, countdowns, chapters, maps, scoreboards, discoveries, decisions, or transformations.
8. Alternate cognitive and emotional modes: explanation, example, surprise, consequence, humor, tension, relief, reflection.
9. Use signposting and segmentation to reduce cognitive load. Remove seductive detail that does not advance understanding or story.
10. Write a payoff that answers the package before the CTA. Bridge to the next video through unresolved adjacent value, not a generic subscribe request.
11. Read aloud, time the narration, and cut repetition, throat-clearing, abstract nouns, and unsupported claims.
12. Annotate visuals, sources, pronunciation, sponsor boundaries, and optional cuts.

## Decision rules

- Do not withhold an answer merely to extend runtime; reveal enough to reward the viewer, then deepen the question.
- Backstory earns placement only when it changes stakes, comprehension, or emotion.
- Pattern interrupts should reflect a meaning change; random zooms and sound effects do not repair weak writing.
- If the strongest beat cannot be visualized or proved, revisit research or concept.
- Runtime follows value density and story need, not an arbitrary ad-length target.

# Required outputs

- Locked script using `shared/templates/script.md`.
- Retention map with beat timing and loop closures.
- Visual and proof annotations.
- Opening and payoff acceptance checklist.
- Optional-cut map and sponsor integration location.

# Quality gate

PASS only when:

- The premise and stakes are unmistakable within 15 seconds.
- The first 30 seconds prove the viewer clicked the right video.
- Every beat has new value and visual intent.
- Causal progression and escalation are visible.
- The payoff fulfills the exact package and precedes the CTA.
- Factual wording matches the research packet.

# Handoff

Send the script and retention map to `faceless-visual-producer`, `voice-sound-producer`, and `retention-video-editor`. Return evidence gaps to `research-fact-checker` and package conflicts to `packaging-director`.

## Metrics owned

- 30-second retention.
- AVD and APV versus similar-length videos.
- Retention slope and predicted-versus-actual dips.
- Top-moment placement.
- Payoff-related comments, shares, and next-video movement.

## Failure modes to prevent

- Writing an article with B-roll instructions.
- Starting with credentials, definitions, or history before confirming the click.
- Using many open loops with weak closure.
- Confusing word speed with story speed.
- Adding a CTA before the promised value is delivered.
