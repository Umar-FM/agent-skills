# Purpose

Determine whether attention is becoming a viable business. Model uncertainty honestly, separate revenue from profit, track cash and contribution by video/format, and guide where to invest or reduce scope.

## Use this skill when

- Creating a channel business plan or production budget.
- Greenlighting expensive concepts or hiring.
- Evaluating sponsors, products, affiliates, or tools.
- Revenue is growing but cash or profit is unclear.

## Required inputs

- Channel strategy, format slate, production estimates, and capacity.
- Actual views, impressions, watch time, revenue by stream, and payment timing.
- Vendor, labor, software, licensing, travel, product, and overhead costs.
- Tax, currency, refund, commission, and working-capital assumptions.
- Risk ranges and scenario inputs.

## Non-negotiable operating rules

- Use ranges and scenarios; do not present guessed RPM or conversion as fact.
- Separate gross revenue, net revenue, variable cost, contribution, overhead, profit, and cash timing.
- Attribute costs consistently and document assumptions.
- Sunk cost does not justify additional spend.
- Optimize long-term library value and strategic learning as well as immediate payback.

# Workflow

1. Create a chart of accounts appropriate to the business: production labor, contractors, assets, software, music, rights, travel, sponsor sales, fulfillment, payment fees, and overhead.
2. Build per-video budgets with low/base/high ranges and stage-specific contingency.
3. Model revenue by source and time horizon: ads/Premium, sponsor, affiliate, product, membership, licensing, and other.
4. Calculate contribution margin, revenue per 1,000 views/impressions, revenue and contribution per production hour, and cash payback.
5. Create format-level P&Ls using cohorts rather than judging only individual outliers.
6. Track forecast versus actual and explain variance: views, RPM, conversion, cost, timing, or scope.
7. Model hiring/outsourcing decisions against recurring volume, quality impact, management overhead, and bottleneck relief.
8. Set greenlight cost bands and escalation thresholds. Use milestone funding for high-variance projects.
9. Monitor revenue concentration, receivables, taxes, reserves, subscriptions, and cash runway.
10. Recommend reinvestment based on expected marginal audience value, revenue, learning, and risk.

## Decision rules

- Do not approve production spend solely because a video might go viral.
- A lower-cost format can be superior even with fewer views if contribution and learning are stronger.
- Sponsor gross fee must be reduced by sales commission, production, usage burden, and opportunity cost.
- Owned products can increase margin but add fulfillment, support, refund, legal, and inventory risk.
- Hiring requires a stable bottleneck and sufficient runway; contractors preserve flexibility during discovery.

# Required outputs

- Channel budget and cash-flow forecast.
- Per-video and per-format P&L.
- Revenue dashboard based on `shared/templates/revenue_dashboard.csv`.
- Hiring, tool, and outsourcing business cases.
- Greenlight cost range and postmortem variance report.

# Quality gate

PASS only when:

- Assumptions, ranges, and sensitivity are explicit.
- Cash timing and gross/net distinctions are correct.
- Costs and revenue are traceable to projects or documented overhead.
- Decision metrics include contribution and production time, not only views.
- Models are updated with actuals and do not preserve disproven assumptions.

# Handoff

Provide cost bands to the orchestrator and operations, deal economics to sponsor/monetization roles, and variance findings to analytics and strategy.

## Metrics owned

- Contribution margin and cash runway.
- Revenue/contribution per production hour.
- Forecast accuracy.
- Payback period and evergreen tail.
- Revenue concentration, receivables, and cost variance.

## Failure modes to prevent

- Using social-media CPM anecdotes as forecasts.
- Calling revenue profit.
- Ignoring the cost of founder/employee time.
- Overbuilding a fixed team before format-market fit.
- Cutting research, rights, or quality controls that prevent catastrophic downside.
