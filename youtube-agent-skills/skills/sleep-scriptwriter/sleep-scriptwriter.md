# Purpose

Write sleep scripts that combine relaxation with enough gentle curiosity to earn the click and sustain background listening. Protect calm pacing, factual integrity, originality, and production continuity.

## Use this skill when

- Writing or rewriting a sleep, calm-history, bedtime-story, ambient documentary, or background-listening script.
- A normal documentary script needs a low-arousal sleep version.
- Hooks, CTAs, visuals, or audio notes need to be softened for sleep continuity.
- A sleep script needs chapter structure, source notes, and policy-safe originality review before production.

## Required inputs

- Sleep channel blueprint or explicit sleep-state promise.
- Locked title/package, topic, audience, target length, and chapter length range.
- Voice card, research packet, source boundaries, and do-not-claim list.
- Ambience, visual style, CTA constraints, and policy notes.

## Non-negotiable operating rules

- The opening confirms the title softly and gives permission to relax.
- Each chapter needs distinct substance, one gentle question, and one soft payoff.
- Curiosity must never become panic, gore, aggressive urgency, or hard cliffhanger pressure.
- Preserve factual meaning and uncertainty; do not invent atmosphere as fact.
- Include visual and audio notes that prevent sudden brightness, loud stings, and jarring cuts.

# Workflow

1. Define the soft promise: what the listener will learn, imagine, or feel while relaxing.
2. Draft the opening hook with title confirmation, low-arousal imagery, and a listener permission line.
3. Build 8-12 minute chapters. For each, name the gentle question, sensory anchor, facts or story points, soft payoff, and transition.
4. Write narration in medium-long, smooth sentences with specific sensory detail and low emotional temperature.
5. Use transitions that make it acceptable to miss details while still rewarding active listeners.
6. Place a soft CTA only after the listener has settled or at the end.
7. Annotate voice direction, ambience, visual direction, source notes, and policy notes.
8. Run a sleep rewrite pass to remove hype, sudden tonal shifts, sharp consonant-heavy phrasing where practical, unsupported claims, and repeated filler.
9. Hand the script to policy, visual, voice, and editing agents with the sleep-specific constraints visible.

## Decision rules

- A sleep script is not a normal documentary with rain added; structure, language, audio, and visuals must all serve low arousal.
- Use gentle mysteries and quiet mechanisms instead of shock reveals.
- If a section needs danger or conflict, narrate it with distance, context, and care.
- Long runtime must come from distinct chapters, not repeated phrasing.
- If factual support is thin, make the section speculative, folkloric, or fictional only with clear labeling.

# Required outputs

- Sleep script package using `shared/SLEEP_CHANNEL_PLAYBOOK.md`.
- Chapter map with gentle questions, sensory anchors, soft payoffs, and transition lines.
- Full narration with voice, visual, ambience, source, and policy annotations.
- Soft CTA and optional-cut notes.
- Sleep rewrite notes and unresolved research questions.

# Quality gate

PASS only when:

- The opening includes soft promise and permission to relax.
- No chapter spikes arousal with sudden danger, gore, yelling, aggressive CTA, or sharp tonal jump.
- Each chapter has distinct value rather than filler repetition.
- Visual and audio notes protect sleep continuity.
- Factual videos include source notes and uncertainty boundaries.

# Handoff

Send final script, chapter map, and constraints to `faceless-visual-producer`, `voice-sound-producer`, `retention-video-editor`, `publishing-seo-distribution`, and `rights-policy-brand-safety`. Return unsupported claims to `research-fact-checker` before production.

## Metrics owned

- First-30-second retention for sleep packages.
- Long-session watch time and average percentage viewed by runtime band.
- Sleep-specific viewer complaints about pacing, volume, repetition, or stimulation.
- Chapter completion and rewatch patterns.
- Policy/originality revision rate before upload.

## Failure modes to prevent

- Writing a normal documentary and adding rain sounds.
- Using crime, horror, or conflict hooks that are too sharp for sleep.
- Repeating the same ambience, chapter structure, and visuals with no substantive variation.
- Inventing ancient secrets, historical certainty, or source claims without evidence.
- Ending with a loud or urgent CTA.
