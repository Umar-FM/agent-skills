# Purpose

Replace imagined viewer personas with evidence. Discover what the audience is trying to feel, learn, solve, compare, avoid, or become; how they describe those needs; what they already watch; and which unmet tensions can support repeatable content and ethical monetization.

## Use this skill when

- Creating or revising the channel thesis.
- Developing a new content pillar, series, offer, or sponsor category.
- Investigating weak audience fit, comments, or returning-viewer performance.
- Searching for durable idea territories rather than one-off trends.

## Required inputs

- Research question and decision it will inform.
- Candidate audience, niche, geography, and language.
- Existing channel analytics, comments, surveys, emails, or community data.
- Competitor and adjacent-channel list.
- Privacy, legal, and data-access constraints.

## Non-negotiable operating rules

- Browse current public sources when market behavior, platform features, trends, or products may have changed.
- Distinguish observed behavior, stated preference, inference, and assumption.
- Do not collect private or sensitive personal data without a legitimate basis and consent.
- Use exact audience language, but do not overgeneralize from a loud minority.
- Research viewing intent and commercial intent separately.

# Workflow

1. Frame 3-7 research questions tied to decisions: audience promise, idea demand, objections, format preference, trust, or monetization.
2. Collect evidence from YouTube search suggestions, Trends/Research insights, comments, competitor videos, forums, product reviews, newsletters, public communities, and existing analytics.
3. Sample across successful, average, and failed content; do not study only viral winners.
4. Code evidence into jobs, pains, desires, anxieties, identity signals, knowledge level, emotional triggers, viewing situations, and purchase intent.
5. Map the audience’s current alternatives: channels, products, communities, and “do nothing.”
6. Identify repeated language patterns and high-energy words that can inform titles and scripts without keyword stuffing.
7. Find unresolved tensions: questions repeatedly asked, explanations viewers distrust, comparisons they cannot make, or outcomes existing videos fail to prove.
8. Segment only when segments require meaningfully different promises, packages, or products.
9. Assign confidence based on source quality, sample diversity, and consistency.
10. Translate findings into strategic implications and testable content hypotheses.

## Decision rules

- A segment is actionable only if it changes content, packaging, distribution, or monetization.
- Comments reveal language and emotion, not population-level prevalence.
- Search volume proves intent exists, not that the channel can win or retain viewers.
- High commercial intent does not justify promotional content that underdelivers on entertainment or education.
- When direct evidence is unavailable, label the assumption and design a low-cost test.

# Required outputs

- Completed `shared/templates/audience_profile.md`.
- Evidence table with source, confidence, and implication.
- Audience language and objection bank.
- Content-gap map and opportunity territories.
- Commercial-intent and sponsor/product-fit notes.

# Quality gate

PASS only when:

- Every important insight has evidence or is explicitly labeled an assumption.
- The profile explains viewing context and desired outcome, not only demographics.
- The language bank contains real recurring phrases with source provenance.
- The gap map can generate multiple concept directions.
- Commercial insights do not compromise the channel promise.

# Handoff

Provide the evidence packet to `channel-strategy-architect`, `idea-format-developer`, `title-copywriter`, `story-retention-scriptwriter`, `community-audience-development`, and monetization roles. Flag freshness dates and unresolved assumptions.

## Metrics owned

- Research insights later validated by analytics or direct feedback.
- Idea hit rate from identified opportunity territories.
- Reduction in audience-fit postmortems.
- Sponsor/product conversion by identified intent.
- Freshness and source-quality coverage.

## Failure modes to prevent

- Inventing a detailed avatar from intuition.
- Using subscriber demographics as a complete audience model.
- Mistaking complaints for the dominant need.
- Copying competitor audiences without identifying an ownable promise.
- Producing research that cannot change a decision.
