# 07 — Drills, Sprints, and Curriculum

## Purpose

Use this file when the user wants to learn the bundle as a skill, build muscle memory, train a team, or run a structured implementation sprint. The bundle is meant to be practiced through outputs, not memorized.

## Training philosophy

A user has learned the skill when they can:

- Diagnose the current bottleneck.
- Select the right module.
- Build a practical artifact.
- Define a test.
- Measure the result.
- Iterate based on evidence.

Do not treat learning as reading. Treat learning as repetitions.

## Level 0 — Business Snapshot

### Objective

Create a clear baseline before solving anything.

### Inputs

- What you sell.
- Who buys.
- Price.
- Monthly revenue.
- Gross margin.
- Lead sources.
- Close rate.
- Delivery process.
- Churn/refunds.
- Team size.
- Main goal.

### Output

One-page business snapshot:

|---|---|

### Drill

Complete the snapshot in 20 minutes. Mark unknown numbers. Unknown numbers become homework.

### Pass standard

The business can be explained in one paragraph, and the current suspected bottleneck is named.

## Level 1 — Offer Builder

### Objective

Create an offer for one buyer, one problem, and one outcome.

### Weekly curriculum

#### Day 1 — Market and buyer

- Pick three possible markets.
- Score each on pain, purchasing power, reachability, and existing spend.
- Select one market.

Deliverable:

- Market scorecard.

#### Day 2 — Buyer problem and dream outcome

- Write the buyer’s before state.
- Write the buyer’s desired after state.
- Write the cost of inaction.

Deliverable:

- Buyer transformation statement.

#### Day 3 — Obstacle map

- List 25 obstacles.
- Convert top 10 into offer components.

Deliverable:

- Obstacle-to-component table.

#### Day 4 — Offer stack

- Create core deliverable.
- Add fast-start component.
- Add implementation support.
- Add proof mechanism.
- Add three bonuses.

Deliverable:

- Offer stack v1.

#### Day 5 — Price and guarantee

- Choose price and terms.
- Write guarantee.
- Stress-test guarantee.

Deliverable:

- Price/terms/guarantee sheet.

#### Day 6 — Naming and sales one-liner

- Write 30 names.
- Pick top three.
- Write one-line offer statement.

Deliverable:

- Offer name and one-liner.

#### Day 7 — Market feedback

- Send to 10 target buyers or knowledgeable referrers.
- Ask what is clear, compelling, unbelievable, or missing.

Deliverable:

- Feedback log and revision.

### Pass standard

The offer can be explained in one sentence, handles the top objections, and has a defined test.

## Level 2 — Lead Operator

### Objective

Generate qualified conversations through one primary channel.

### 30-day lead sprint

#### Week 1 — Channel and asset setup

- Confirm target buyer and offer.
- Choose primary channel.
- Create lead magnet or direct CTA.
- Build tracking sheet.
- Write scripts/hooks.

Deliverables:

- One-page advertising plan.
- Lead magnet/CTA.
- Tracking sheet.
- Script set.

#### Week 2 — Volume sprint

- Warm outreach: 20/day, or
- Cold outreach: 25/day, or
- Content: 1–3 posts/day plus 10 conversations/day, or
- Paid ads: launch 3–5 creatives.

Deliverables:

- Daily activity log.
- Reply/booking data.
- Objection log.

#### Week 3 — Conversion improvement

- Improve CTA.
- Improve follow-up.
- Improve booking flow.
- Add proof.
- Handle common objections.

Deliverables:

- Revised scripts/assets.
- Follow-up sequence.
- Proof asset.

#### Week 4 — Decide and systematize

- Compare results to success threshold.
- Keep, kill, or iterate channel.
- Document process.
- Decide next test.

Deliverables:

- Channel report.
- SOP v1.
- Next 30-day plan.

### Daily lead drills

- Write 10 hooks.
- Send 10–25 direct messages.
- Comment meaningfully on 10 target-buyer posts.
- Add 10 prospects to list.
- Follow up with five open conversations.
- Extract one objection or phrase from a prospect.

## Level 3 — Money Model Designer

### Objective

Improve gross profit, payback, and customer value with a clear offer stack.

### 14-day money model sprint

#### Day 1 — Current model map

- List every offer.
- List price, conversion, margin, and cash timing.

#### Day 2 — Unit economics

- Calculate CAC, gross profit/customer, payback, refund, churn, and cash collected in first 30 days.

#### Day 3 — Constraint diagnosis

- Identify whether the issue is CAC, close rate, gross margin, payback, churn, refund, cash collection, or capacity.

#### Day 4 — Attraction offer ideas

- Create 10 possible attraction offers.
- Score buyer value, conversion, margin, and core-offer fit.

#### Day 5 — Core offer improvement

- Improve price, terms, stack, onboarding, or qualification.

#### Day 6 — Upsell map

- Create faster, easier, deeper, and premium upsells.

#### Day 7 — Downsell map

- Create downsells for price, timing, trust, and effort objections.

#### Day 8 — Continuity map

- Create recurring value layer.

#### Day 9 — Cash conversion

- Identify setup fees, deposits, prepay, payment terms, or paid diagnostic opportunities.

#### Day 10 — Fulfillment margin

- Remove, automate, template, group, or reprice low-margin delivery pieces.

#### Day 11 — Forecast

- Build simple current vs target table.

#### Day 12 — Test design

- Select one offer-stack change to test.

#### Day 13 — Sales/asset update

- Update sales script, page, checkout, or follow-up.

#### Day 14 — Launch

- Start the test and define reporting cadence.

### Pass standard

The user can explain how a customer moves through the offer stack and how each step affects gross profit, CAC, payback, retention, and customer success.

## Level 4 — Scaling Operator

### Objective

Identify and remove the current scaling bottleneck.

### 30-day scaling sprint

#### Week 1 — Diagnose

- Complete business snapshot.
- Map funnel from lead to cash to retention.
- Stress-test what breaks if volume doubles.
- Identify top bottlenecks.

Deliverables:

- Scaling diagnosis.
- KPI tree.
- Bottleneck queue.

#### Week 2 — Prioritize

- Score bottleneck projects.
- Pick top one to three.
- Define owners, metrics, and deadlines.
- Kill distractions.

Deliverables:

- 90-day objective.
- Project scorecard.
- Owner map.

#### Week 3 — Build system

- Create SOPs.
- Create dashboard.
- Create meeting cadence.
- Create hiring/delegation plan if needed.

Deliverables:

- SOP v1.
- Dashboard.
- Weekly agenda.

#### Week 4 — Run cadence

- Hold weekly scorecard review.
- Review project progress.
- Resolve blockers.
- Decide continue/kill/iterate.

Deliverables:

- First weekly review report.
- Updated roadmap.

### Pass standard

The user can state the current constraint, show evidence, name the owner, define the metric, and run a weekly cadence around fixing it.

## Implementation sprints

## 7-Day Offer Sprint

### Day 1 — Market decision

Pick one buyer and problem.

### Day 2 — Value Equation audit

Score dream outcome, likelihood, time delay, and effort/sacrifice.

### Day 3 — Obstacle-to-component map

Create offer components.

### Day 4 — Stack and price

Build the package and terms.

### Day 5 — Bonuses and guarantee

Add objection-removal bonuses and risk reversal.

### Day 6 — Name and asset

Create name, one-liner, one-page offer.

### Day 7 — Feedback test

Send to 10 target buyers.

## 7-Day Lead Sprint

### Day 1 — Choose channel and CTA

Pick one channel and create one CTA.

### Day 2 — Build list or content bank

Create 100 prospects or 30 hooks.

### Day 3 — Send/publish

Start volume.

### Day 4 — Follow up

Continue volume and follow-up.

### Day 5 — Improve message

Use objections and replies to rewrite.

### Day 6 — Book calls

Push conversations to a clear next step.

### Day 7 — Score and decide

Keep, kill, or iterate.

## 7-Day Money Model Sprint

### Day 1 — Map current offers

Create offer stack table.

### Day 2 — Calculate economics

CAC, gross profit, payback, churn, refunds.

### Day 3 — Pick constraint

Choose one economic bottleneck.

### Day 4 — Create stack improvement

Attraction, upsell, downsell, continuity, price, or terms.

### Day 5 — Create asset/script

Update page, script, or checkout.

### Day 6 — Launch test

Expose to a defined sample.

### Day 7 — Review early signals

Adjust or continue.

## 7-Day Scaling Sprint

### Day 1 — Stage and constraint diagnosis

Classify stage and current bottleneck.

### Day 2 — KPI tree

Map lead to cash to retention.

### Day 3 — Double-volume stress test

Identify first break point.

### Day 4 — Bottleneck project scoring

Score and pick top projects.

### Day 5 — Owner and cadence

Assign owner and weekly meeting.

### Day 6 — SOP/dashboard

Create v1 process and dashboard.

### Day 7 — Run first review

Review metrics and next actions.

## 90-day implementation roadmap

### Month 1 — Clarity and validation

- Offer rebuild.
- First acquisition channel.
- Initial proof.
- Basic tracking.

### Month 2 — Economics and conversion

- Lead channel optimization.
- Sales script and follow-up.
- Money model map.

### Month 3 — Systems and scale

- Stage diagnosis.
- Bottleneck project.
- SOPs.

## Practice prompts

Use these prompts to train the agent or user.

### Offer prompts

- “Audit this offer using the Value Equation and rebuild it.”
- “Create three premium offer versions for this niche.”
- “Turn these objections into bonuses and guarantees.”
- “Write 30 names for this productized service.”

### Lead prompts

- “Create a 30-day lead plan using warm outreach and content.”
- “Build a lead magnet for this offer.”
- “Write a cold outreach sequence for this buyer.”
- “Design a paid ad mini-test with success thresholds.”

### Money model prompts

- “Map my current money model and find the constraint.”
- “Design an attraction offer, upsell, downsell, and continuity plan.”
- “Calculate payback using these numbers and recommend what to fix.”
- “Create a customer-funded acquisition path.”

### Scaling prompts

- “Diagnose my scaling stage and bottleneck.”
- “Build a 90-day scaling roadmap.”
- “Create a KPI tree for this business.”
- “What breaks if we double sales?”

## Certification-style evaluation

A user/operator passes the bundle when they can complete all of the following:

1. Build a business snapshot from messy context.
2. Diagnose the likely bottleneck.
3. Rebuild an offer using Value Equation logic.
4. Create a 30-day lead plan for one channel.
5. Write scripts/assets for the lead plan.
6. Map a money model and calculate basic economics.
7. Propose one offer-stack improvement.
8. Diagnose scaling stage and first failure point.
9. Build a 90-day roadmap with owners and metrics.
10. Define a test, success threshold, kill condition, and iteration rule.

## Team training format

For a team, run a weekly workshop:

### 15 minutes — Metrics

Review dashboard and constraint.

### 20 minutes — Concept

Teach one framework.

### 30 minutes — Build

Create one artifact.

### 20 minutes — Red team

Use the rubric to critique.

### 15 minutes — Commit

Define owner, metric, deadline.

## Red-team prompts

Use these before implementation:

- Why might this fail?
- What assumption is most fragile?
- What would the buyer not believe?
- What would make fulfillment break?
- What legal/compliance risk exists?
- What metric could look good while the business gets worse?
- What are we ignoring because it is uncomfortable?
- What is the simplest smaller test?

## Final rule

A skill is not installed until the user has run at least one real test and reviewed the data. Every sprint should end with a decision: keep, kill, or iterate.
