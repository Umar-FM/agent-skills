# Purpose

Use Shorts as a distinct discovery and testing format, not as automatic crops. Compress one clear promise into an immediate, complete experience while preserving originality and creating an optional path to deeper long-form value.

## Use this skill when

- A long-form project contains short, standalone moments.
- The channel needs top-of-funnel experiments or faster idea testing.
- A Shorts-native format or series is being developed.
- Viewed-versus-swiped, completion, or repeat performance is weak.

## Required inputs

- Long-form script/cut or Shorts-native concept.
- Target viewer, first-frame visual, and central payoff.
- Rights and music constraints.
- Channel bridge objective and related long-form destination.
- Past Shorts analytics.

## Non-negotiable operating rules

- A Short must make sense without seeing the long-form video.
- The first frame and first spoken line must communicate subject and tension immediately.
- Do not preserve long-form setup, aspect ratio, or pacing by default.
- Loops should feel rewarding, not manipulative or confusing.
- Repurposing still requires original editorial value and materially varied substance.

# Workflow

1. Select one idea, result, question, comparison, transformation, or moment. Reject clips that need extensive context.
2. Write the first-frame promise and first sentence together. Remove greetings, logos, and scene-setting.
3. Structure a compressed causal arc: setup, escalation/evidence, payoff, and optional loop/bridge.
4. Reframe visually for vertical viewing with readable subjects and text; create new graphics rather than merely cropping important information.
5. Cut at the meaning level. Maintain clarity at high speed and use captions as comprehension support.
6. Design a satisfying ending. A loop may return visually or verbally to the opening, but the viewer should still receive the promised value.
7. Use music and assets that are permitted for the exact Shorts length and distribution context.
8. Create multiple opening variants for future uploads rather than reposting identical content.
9. Add a related-video bridge only when the long-form video genuinely deepens the same desire.
10. Review viewed-versus-swiped, engaged views, AVD, APV, repeats, comments, subscribers, and long-form movement.

## Decision rules

- Choose Shorts-native concepts when long-form extraction loses the central tension.
- High completion on a very short clip is not enough; inspect meaningful watch time, rewatches, satisfaction, and audience fit.
- A Short that attracts a different audience from the long-form channel needs its own strategy or should not be used as a bridge.
- Do not force every long-form upload into multiple Shorts; select only moments with standalone value.
- Treat Shorts ideas as tests, but validate whether the same framing transfers to long-form.

# Required outputs

- Shorts brief with first-frame and payoff.
- Vertical script/cut list and visual reframe plan.
- Rights/music check and caption file.
- Related-video bridge plan.
- Shorts postmortem and long-form transfer hypothesis.

# Quality gate

PASS only when:

- The subject and tension are clear in the first second.
- The Short delivers a complete payoff.
- Vertical framing and captions remain readable.
- The asset is original and rights-cleared.
- Any long-form bridge is relevant and measurable.

# Handoff

Send production needs to visual/audio/editor roles, publish assets to `publishing-seo-distribution`, rights to `rights-policy-brand-safety`, and results to `analytics-experimentation-scientist` and `idea-format-developer`.

## Metrics owned

- Viewed versus swiped away.
- Engaged views, AVD, APV, and repeats.
- Subscriber and returning-viewer quality.
- Related-video clicks and long-form conversion.
- Performance of opening and format hypotheses.

## Failure modes to prevent

- Posting random highlights without context.
- Using captions as clutter or duplicating every word with oversized text.
- Ending before the promised answer to force a loop.
- Assuming Shorts subscribers will automatically watch long-form.
- Mass-producing nearly identical template clips.
