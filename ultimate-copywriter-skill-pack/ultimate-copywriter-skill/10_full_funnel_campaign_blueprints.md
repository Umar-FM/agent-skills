# 10 — Full-Funnel Campaign Blueprints

This module shows how to turn strategy into complete campaigns. Use these blueprints when the user wants more than a single asset: a launch, funnel, demand campaign, lifecycle sequence, or go-to-market messaging system.

## Full-funnel principle

A funnel is not a pile of assets. It is a sequence of decisions. Each asset should move the buyer from one mental state to the next.

Typical states:

1. Not aware.
2. Recognizes problem.
3. Understands cost/stakes.
4. Believes a new way is needed.
5. Trusts the mechanism.
6. Believes the product/offer can help.
7. Feels safe taking the next step.
8. Acts.
9. Activates or attends.
10. Continues, buys, expands, refers.

Audience: founders and marketing leads with vague homepages.

Campaign idea:

> **Your homepage may not need a redesign. It may need a better sales argument.**

### Funnel map

| Stage | Asset | CTA |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | LinkedIn posts, teardown clips | Read/watch |
| Interest | Homepage checklist | Download |
| Consideration | Live teardown webinar | Attend |
| Conversion | Sprint landing page | Apply |
| Sales | Application review + audit call | Book sprint |

### Social post series

Post 1 — core belief:

> Your homepage may not need a redesign.
>
> It may need a better sales argument.
>
> Before changing layout, answer:
> - Who is this for?
> - What problem does it solve?
> - What changes after someone uses it?
> - Why should they believe you?
> - What should they do next?
>
> If the page misses those, design will mostly make the confusion prettier.

Post 2 — teardown principle:

> The fastest homepage test:
>
> Read only the hero section.
>
> Can you identify the buyer, outcome, mechanism, and CTA?
>
> If not, the rest of the page is carrying too much weight.

Post 3 — proof:

> Proof usually appears too late.
>
> If the hero makes a major promise, the next section should not be another vague benefit. It should show why the promise is believable.

## Full-funnel QA checklist

Before launching any funnel, confirm:

- Every asset has one job.
- The message matches the buyer's awareness stage.
- The lead magnet leads naturally to the offer.
- The landing page matches the ad/email promise.
- The email sequence progresses belief, proof, and action.
- Retargeting addresses real friction.
- Sales enablement supports internal persuasion.
- The offer and CTA match readiness.
- Measurement tracks quality, not just volume.
- Claims are substantiated.
- Urgency is real.

## Final funnel rule

A funnel should feel like a helpful sequence of increasingly specific decisions. If it feels like a series of disconnected promotions, rebuild the buyer journey before rewriting the copy.
