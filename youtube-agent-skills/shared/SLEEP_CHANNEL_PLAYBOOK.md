# Sleep Channel Playbook

Use this for sleep, calm history, bedtime story, ambient documentary, meditation-adjacent, and background-listening channels. Sleep is a viewer state, not just a topic.

## Operating thesis

Sleep content should repeat a calming promise, not repeat the same substance. The safer channel is an authored calm documentary or story property with original scripts, varied subjects, source-backed context where factual, and recognizable but flexible packaging.

## Strategy rules

- Define the sleep-state promise: what the viewer feels and why they return.
- Pair topic with emotional packaging, such as calm mystery, cozy history, cosmic wonder, rainy folklore, or quiet nature.
- Choose one dominant content engine, usually invisible force, buried detail, unseen system, chain reaction, or gentle mystery.
- Define both repetition and variation. Rotate eras, places, objects, ambience, chapter structures, visual motifs, and source types.
- Avoid generic AI narration over repeated black screens or identical visual loops.
- Add educational context and source notes in descriptions for factual episodes.

## Script rules

1. Open with a soft promise that confirms the title and gives permission to drift in and out.
2. Use chapters every 8-12 minutes, each with one gentle question and one soft payoff.
3. Use sensory anchors: weather, light, texture, distance, footsteps, maps, rooms, sky, water, fabric, or quiet tools.
4. Keep sentence rhythm medium-long and smooth. Avoid hype cadence, aggressive questions, sudden danger, and hard pivots.
5. For mysteries, frame curiosity as quiet exploration. Avoid gore, panic, and cliffhanger pressure.
6. Use transitions that make it acceptable to miss details.
7. Place a soft CTA only after the listener is settled or at the end.
8. Do not invent factual claims for atmosphere. Keep uncertainty and source boundaries intact.

## Visual and audio rules

- Standard faceless videos can use frequent contrast resets. Sleep videos need slow visual novelty without arousal spikes.
- Add gentle novelty every 20-60 seconds through slow motion, angle changes, map movement, dim texture shifts, or chapter imagery.
- Avoid sudden brightness, fast cuts, startling imagery, thunder cracks, loud stings, and music swells.
- Use stable narration volume, soft de-essing, smooth pauses, room tone or ambience, and no loud CTA.

## Example transformation

Weak topic: `Ancient Rome Sleep Story`

Stronger package:

- Behavior loop: sleep plus curiosity.
- Emotional packaging: calm mystery.
- Content engine: invisible force.
- Story format: hidden truth or buried detail.
- Title: `Rainy Night History: The Hidden Rooms, Roads, and Rituals of Rome`.
- Soft hook: `Tonight, we are going to walk through ancient Rome after the noise has faded, when the markets are closed, the lamps are low, and the city becomes easier to hear.`

## Policy risks

Reject or revise sleep-channel plans that depend on:

- repeated black-screen or near-identical AI-template uploads;
- same title formula, visuals, chapters, and ambience across many videos;
- low-effort reused public-domain compilations without commentary or synthesis;
- unlicensed music, copied voices, copied thumbnails, or copied scripts;
- factual descriptions without source context.
