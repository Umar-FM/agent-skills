# Quality Gates

## Gate 1: Channel fit

PASS only when the video serves a named audience, channel promise, content pillar, and business objective. A high-view idea that trains the wrong audience is not automatically a good channel idea.

## Gate 2: Concept strength

PASS when:

- the idea is clear in one sentence;
- the viewer understands why it is interesting now;
- there is a concrete answer, result, transformation, reveal, or resolution;
- the concept can sustain the intended runtime without padding;
- the channel has credible proof or access;
- production and rights risk are acceptable.

## Gate 3: Packaging honesty and potency

PASS when three distinct package hypotheses exist and each:

- is understood in roughly one second;
- contains one dominant visual idea;
- creates curiosity without withholding basic comprehension;
- complements rather than repeats the title;
- can be paid off by the actual video;
- is legible on mobile;
- is differentiated from close competitors.

## Gate 4: Research integrity

PASS when material claims have appropriate sources, uncertainty is labeled, counterevidence is considered, and every external asset has a planned legal basis.

## Gate 5: Retention architecture

PASS when:

- the first five seconds confirm the click;
- the first 30 seconds contain stakes and progress;
- the video uses causal progression;
- compelling proof is not unnecessarily delayed;
- open loops have scheduled closure;
- each segment changes knowledge, progress, stakes, emotion, or perspective;
- the ending delivers the core payoff before any call to action.

## Gate 6: Faceless production originality

PASS when the channel's original contribution is obvious through narration, analysis, visuals, experiments, sequencing, or perspective. Generic stock montages, readings of existing material, and templated AI output fail.

## Gate 7: Publish safety

PASS when rights, AI disclosure, paid-promotion disclosure, advertiser suitability, captions, audio levels, chapters, end screens, links, claims, and metadata are checked.

## Gate 8: Learning readiness

PASS when the team has recorded:

- primary hypothesis;
- expected performance band;
- target traffic surface;
- planned A/B variants;
- intervention rules;
- analytics review windows;
- revenue attribution method.

A video without a learning plan may still publish, but the orchestrator must document why.
