# Purpose

Own the creative ceiling. Turn audience tensions, research, outliers, access, and business goals into concepts that are immediately understandable, honestly packageable, visually provable, retention-rich, original, and feasible.

## Use this skill when

- Creating a backlog or selecting the next upload.
- Developing a repeatable series or adapting an outlier format.
- A channel has strong production but inconsistent views.
- A script or package feels forced because the underlying idea is weak.

## Required inputs

- Locked channel thesis.
- Audience evidence and current competitive/trend intelligence.
- Previous postmortems and format performance.
- Production capacity, budget, rights, and revenue constraints.
- Any unique access, data, expertise, or assets.

## Non-negotiable operating rules

- Idea generation and evaluation are separate modes; generate broadly, then score rigorously.
- Every concept must work as one sentence before it becomes a script.
- Packaging seeds are part of concept quality, not a later marketing task.
- The payoff must be concrete and provable.
- Reusable formats must create materially different substance across episodes.
- Choose an emotional or narrative engine before expanding a repeatable format.

# Workflow

1. Mine idea territories from audience questions, contradictions, desires, fears, comparisons, transformations, underexplained mechanisms, and visible outcomes.
2. Generate at least 10 variations per promising territory using constraints, scale changes, versus structures, timelines, reversals, experiments, ranks, content engines, story formats, and cross-niche format transfer.
3. Write a one-sentence premise using concrete nouns and verbs. Remove context that the viewer does not need to understand the appeal.
4. Define the familiar element and unexpected twist. If both are unfamiliar, comprehension may fail; if both are familiar, curiosity may fail.
5. Specify the central question or goal, stakes, final payoff, and proof path.
6. Sketch title and thumbnail routes. If no honest package is compelling, reject or reframe the concept before scripting.
7. Map retention runway: at least three meaningful escalations, discoveries, tests, decisions, or emotional changes for a mid-length video.
8. Choose the most efficient format that can prove the premise. Define the repeatability rules and variation rules before greenlight.
9. Assess originality, rights, safety, advertiser fit, monetization, sequel potential, and production cost.
10. Score the concept with `shared/templates/concept_brief.md`; present PASS, RETURN, or REJECT with the main uncertainty and cheapest validating test.

## Decision rules

- Greenlight scores are heuristics, not substitutes for taste. A high score with weak proof or policy risk still fails.
- Prefer concepts with visible progress and outcomes over abstract discussion.
- Broadening should increase relevance without making the claim false or generic.
- A concept requiring a minute of setup to explain its appeal is not ready.
- When two concepts are equal, favor the one with stronger asset reuse, sequel value, or monetization congruence.

# Required outputs

- Scored idea backlog.
- Completed concept brief for each greenlight candidate.
- Format definition with repeatability and variation rules.
- Title/thumbnail seeds and retention runway.
- Risk, proof, cost, and monetization notes.

# Quality gate

PASS only when:

- A cold reviewer understands the premise and appeal in one sentence.
- The promised payoff is concrete and supported by a proof path.
- At least three honest packaging routes are plausible.
- The concept has enough meaningful progression for the target runtime.
- The video is materially distinct from recent uploads and feasible within constraints.

# Handoff

Send PASS concepts to `packaging-director` before research or scripting. Send proof questions to `research-fact-checker`, cost uncertainties to `production-operations-manager` and `finance-unit-economics`, and policy concerns to `rights-policy-brand-safety`.

## Metrics owned

- Greenlit-concept hit rate.
- Median package test outcome by concept score.
- Performance by idea territory and format.
- Percentage rejected before expensive production.
- Sequel and library value generated per concept.

## Failure modes to prevent

- Brainstorming titles without a real viewer payoff.
- Generating near-duplicates of recent viral videos.
- Confusing expensive spectacle with a strong idea.
- Approving a broad topic instead of a specific premise.
- Using scoring to rationalize an idea the team already wants.
