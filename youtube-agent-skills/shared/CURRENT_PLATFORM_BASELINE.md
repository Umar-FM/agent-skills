# Current Platform Baseline

**Verification date: 2026-06-23. Recheck official YouTube documentation before consequential use.**

## Native title and thumbnail testing

YouTube currently documents native A/B testing for up to three titles, thumbnails, or title-thumbnail combinations on eligible long-form videos. Tests are desktop-only, can take up to two weeks, and select a winner using **watch-time share**, not CTR alone. Shorts, scheduled live streams, and Premiere videos are not eligible at the documented time; eligible live archives and videos after a Premiere can be tested.

Source: [A/B test titles and thumbnails](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/16391400?hl=en)

## CTR interpretation

YouTube states that CTR varies by content, audience, and surface. Half of channels and videos may fall in a broad 2%-10% impressions CTR range, but this is not a target. Home distribution commonly lowers CTR as reach expands. Evaluate CTR with impressions, traffic source, AVD, and audience composition.

Source: [Impressions and CTR FAQs](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/7628154?hl=en)

## Audience retention

The retention report highlights intros, top moments, spikes, and dips. Intro is the percentage still watching after the first 30 seconds. YouTube recommends aligning the opening with title/thumbnail expectations and moving compelling later moments earlier where appropriate.

Source: [Audience retention key moments](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/9314415?hl=en)

## YPP thresholds

The standard ad-revenue eligibility path is currently documented as either:

- 1,000 subscribers plus 4,000 valid public watch hours in the last 12 months; or
- 1,000 subscribers plus 10 million valid public Shorts views in the last 90 days.

In eligible regions, earlier expanded-YPP access to fan funding and select Shopping features is currently documented at:

- 500 subscribers, three valid public uploads in the last 90 days, and 3,000 valid public watch hours in the last 12 months; or
- 500 subscribers, three valid public uploads in the last 90 days, and 3 million valid public Shorts views in the last 90 days.

Sources: [YPP overview](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/72851?hl=en) and [expanded YPP](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/13429240?hl=en)

## Originality and inauthentic content

YouTube's July 2025 policy clarification renamed “repetitious content” to **inauthentic content** and explicitly describes repetitive, mass-produced, template-like, and generic AI content without original insight as ineligible for monetization. Reused content is separately assessed and requires meaningful difference, commentary, substantive modification, or educational/entertainment value. Permission alone does not resolve reused-content eligibility.

Source: [Channel monetization policies](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/1311392?hl=en)

## AI and paid promotion

Creators must use current YouTube disclosure tools when realistic content has been meaningfully altered or synthetically generated under the platform's rules. Paid product placements, sponsorships, endorsements, and relevant commercial relationships require current platform and legal disclosure.

Sources: [GenAI disclosure](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/14328491?hl=en) and [paid promotions](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/154235?hl=en)
