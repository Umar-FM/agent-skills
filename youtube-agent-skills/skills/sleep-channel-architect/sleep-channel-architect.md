# Purpose

Define sleep channels that can scale without becoming generic AI-template factories. Translate a topic area into a viewer-state promise, repeatable emotional packaging, variation rules, production specifications, and policy-safe topic map.

## Use this skill when

- Launching or repositioning a sleep, calm-history, bedtime-story, ambient documentary, or meditation-adjacent channel.
- A channel needs long-session viewing without repetitive-template risk.
- Sleep videos are too generic, too stimulating, or too similar across uploads.
- A 10-, 15-, or 30-video validation slate is needed for a sleep channel.

## Required inputs

- Channel goal, sleep subniche, target audience, and risk tolerance.
- Production budget, voice type, visual budget, and upload cadence.
- Competitive or outlier research where available.
- Policy, rights, AI disclosure, and monetization constraints.

## Non-negotiable operating rules

- Treat sleep as a viewer state, not a topic label.
- Repeat the calming promise while varying subjects, research angles, visuals, ambience, and chapter shapes.
- Define original authorship: voice, sourcing, point of view, visual grammar, and editorial standards.
- Avoid black-screen, slideshow, reused-audio, or mass-template production models.
- Build policy review into pre-production and pre-upload gates.

# Workflow

1. Define the sleep-state promise: what the viewer feels, why they return, and how much attention they are expected to give.
2. Choose emotional packaging such as calm mystery, cozy history, cosmic wonder, rainy folklore, quiet nature, or gentle learning.
3. Select a dominant content engine and supporting story formats using the pattern mining playbook.
4. Specify narrator persona, sentence rhythm, ambience system, visual motif, chapter style, CTA policy, and source-note policy.
5. Design variation rules across eras, places, objects, story types, visual motifs, ambience, and research sources.
6. Create a 30-video map with distinct subjects, soft hooks, unique research angles, visual variations, and policy risk notes.
7. Define production specs for length, chapter range, narration, ambience, visuals, metadata, and review cadence.
8. Map monetization paths that fit relaxed viewer intent without damaging trust or sleep continuity.
9. Hand the approved blueprint to research, scripting, visual, voice, publishing, and policy agents.

## Decision rules

- Reject channel concepts selected only for rumored RPM, low competition, or cheap production.
- Favor evergreen, repeatable viewer states over one-off topics.
- If two planned videos would have the same title shape, visual motif, ambience, and chapter arc, redesign one before production.
- Use horror, crime, conflict, or danger only when the emotional temperature remains sleep-compatible.
- Preserve educational or narrative value in factual sleep videos; do not rely on ambience alone.

# Required outputs

- Sleep channel blueprint using `shared/SLEEP_CHANNEL_PLAYBOOK.md`.
- 30-video validation map with variation rules.
- Brand, voice, ambience, visual, and metadata standards.
- Policy-safety and originality rules.
- Monetization path and risk review cadence.

# Quality gate

PASS only when:

- The sleep promise is clear in one sentence.
- Repetition and variation are both explicit.
- Every mapped video has a unique research or story angle.
- Production specs prevent sudden arousal spikes and generic template reuse.
- Policy-safety rules cover originality, rights, AI, metadata, and repeated-substance risk.

# Handoff

Send the blueprint to `channel-strategy-architect` for portfolio alignment, `research-fact-checker` for factual source planning, `sleep-scriptwriter` for scripts, `faceless-visual-producer` and `voice-sound-producer` for production systems, `publishing-seo-distribution` for metadata standards, and `rights-policy-brand-safety` for governance.

## Metrics owned

- Returning viewers and long-session watch time by sleep format.
- Repetition complaints and policy-risk findings.
- Topic-map completion and distinct-substance ratio.
- Sleep-channel packaging CTR with 30-second retention.
- Performance by ambience, story format, and subject cluster.

## Failure modes to prevent

- Building a black-screen generic AI-story factory.
- Using the same title formula, visuals, ambience, and story shape for every upload.
- Ignoring source context in factual sleep videos.
- Making the content too sharp, loud, or urgent for sleep use.
- Letting monetization segments break listener trust or relaxation.
