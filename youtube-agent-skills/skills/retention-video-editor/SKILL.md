# Purpose

Turn script, visuals, and sound into the strongest possible viewing experience. Remove confusion and dead time, move compelling proof earlier, maintain progress and emotional variation, and protect the ending payoff.

## Use this skill when

- Assets and narration are ready for rough cut.
- A rough cut feels slow, confusing, repetitive, or overstimulating.
- Past videos show intro drops, mid-video dips, or sponsor-related abandonment.
- A final retention and publish-readiness review is required.

## Required inputs

- Locked package, script, retention map, storyboard, assets, narration, and sponsor requirements.
- Research packet and rights manifest.
- Target runtime and previous retention postmortems.
- Brand editing grammar and delivery specifications.

## Non-negotiable operating rules

- Edit story before polish.
- The first frame and first five seconds must confirm the package.
- Every cut must clarify, prove, intensify, surprise, orient, create emotion, or advance progress.
- Cut dead time, not necessary comprehension or emotional resonance.
- Do not use random zooms, subtitles, memes, or SFX as a substitute for narrative change.

# Workflow

1. Watch all material and assemble the simplest story that fulfills the package. Do not begin with effects.
2. Build the cold open first. Confirm subject, stakes, and proof immediately; eliminate logos, greetings, and redundant setup.
3. Create a story-only rough cut. Test whether a cold viewer can explain the premise, progress, and payoff without polish.
4. Run a confusion pass: missing context, unclear pronouns, unproved claims, unreadable graphics, rushed logic, or unexplained stakes.
5. Run a momentum pass: remove repetition, move strong moments earlier, compress transitions, and add progress markers where the story genuinely advances.
6. Run an emotion pass: preserve contrast among tension, humor, wonder, relief, intimacy, and reflection. Let high-value scenes breathe.
7. Run a visual-proof pass: ensure every material narration claim has the right evidence and that reenactments or simulations are labeled where needed.
8. Integrate sponsor content after the first meaningful payoff where possible, maintain relevance, and avoid restarting the video’s energy.
9. Complete music, SFX, captions, graphics, color, and mix only after the story cut passes.
10. Conduct a cold-viewer review and predicted-dip review using `shared/templates/edit_review.md`.
11. Perform final rights, factual, disclosure, technical, end-screen, and export checks.

## Decision rules

- A longer cut is better when every minute adds value; a shorter cut is better when runtime comes from repetition or delay.
- Use visual change when the viewer’s mental model, location, evidence, emotion, or story state changes.
- Spikes in past videos can represent value or confusion; consult comments and context before copying the pattern.
- Do not hide sponsor content deceptively. Make it concise, relevant, and clearly disclosed.
- If the payoff is weak, return to script or production rather than masking it with a montage.

# Required outputs

- Story rough cut and review notes.
- Completed `shared/templates/edit_review.md`.
- Final master, clean master, captions, stems, and project archive.
- Predicted retention risks and optional recut plan.
- Final asset usage report.

# Quality gate

PASS only when:

- A cold viewer identifies the premise and stakes within 15 seconds.
- No segment survives solely because it was expensive to produce.
- Proof, graphics, narration, and package agree.
- The video has meaningful pace and emotional variation without exhaustion.
- The promised payoff is complete before CTA/end-screen routing.
- Technical, rights, and disclosure checks pass.

# Handoff

Send the final cut and review to `rights-policy-brand-safety`, `research-fact-checker`, and `publishing-seo-distribution`. Send unresolved package mismatch to `packaging-director` and structural problems to `story-retention-scriptwriter`.

## Metrics owned

- 30-second retention.
- AVD/APV and relative retention.
- Dips at transitions, sponsor, CTA, and end.
- Time removed from rough to final cut.
- Cold-review comprehension and satisfaction.

## Failure modes to prevent

- Editing for constant stimulation.
- Keeping scenes because of sunk production cost.
- Delaying the best proof for a late reveal that does not require secrecy.
- Adding captions and B-roll without solving confusion.
- Ending with a long summary and multiple calls to action after the payoff.
