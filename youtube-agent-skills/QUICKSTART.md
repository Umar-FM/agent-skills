# Quickstart: First Channel and First Ten Videos

## 1. Create the business thesis

Have `channel-strategy-architect` complete `shared/templates/channel_thesis.md`. It must define:

- one primary viewer;
- one repeatable emotional and practical promise;
- three to five content pillars;
- the browse/search mix;
- the monetization ladder;
- production limits;
- clear anti-goals.

## 2. Build an evidence-backed idea pool

Use `audience-market-researcher`, `trend-competitive-intelligence`, and `idea-format-developer` to create at least 50 raw ideas, then score them with the shared scorecard. Greenlight only ideas with:

- immediate one-sentence comprehension;
- a visible or demonstrable payoff;
- sufficient curiosity and stakes;
- enough progression for the intended duration;
- a production path that preserves originality;
- plausible revenue and advertiser fit.

## 3. Package before scripting

The `packaging-director` must create three meaningfully different title-thumbnail hypotheses before the script is approved. The concept is not greenlit if no honest, compelling package exists.

## 4. Write to the viewing contract

The first 30 seconds should normally contain:

- **0-5 seconds:** unmistakable confirmation of the title/thumbnail promise;
- **5-15 seconds:** stakes, consequence, tension, or a vivid unanswered question;
- **15-30 seconds:** mechanism, progress, and the first proof or micro-payoff.

Then use causal progression: **because / but / therefore**, not a loose chain of “and then.”

## 5. Produce a faceless video with authorship

Show original participation through distinctive research, narration, analysis, visuals, demonstrations, humor, sequencing, and conclusions. Maintain an asset-rights ledger. Do not rely on generic stock footage as the substance of the video.

## 6. Edit for comprehension and momentum

Every cut must serve at least one job: clarify, prove, intensify, surprise, reset attention, create emotion, or advance the story. Random motion is not retention strategy.

## 7. Publish with a measurement plan

Record the pre-publish hypothesis, target surface, predicted performance band, and planned interventions. Use native title/thumbnail testing where eligible. Do not judge CTR without traffic-source context or adequate impressions.

## 8. Review at four windows

- **Early read:** packaging and technical issues only.
- **24-48 hours:** surface-level CTR, intro retention, obvious dips.
- **7 days:** audience fit, Suggested/Search behavior, returning viewers, revenue quality.
- **28 days:** evergreen tail, series value, contribution margin, reusable lessons.

## 9. Diagnose one bottleneck

Choose one primary diagnosis: idea, audience fit, packaging, promise match, opening, structure, pacing, proof, satisfaction, distribution, monetization, or cost. Do not hide behind “the algorithm.”

## 10. Compound the learning

Convert every postmortem into a rule, test, or reusable asset. The first ten videos are a structured discovery sprint, not ten unrelated bets.
