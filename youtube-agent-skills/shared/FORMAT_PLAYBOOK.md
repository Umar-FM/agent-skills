# Faceless Format Playbook

These formats combine production leverage with strong click-and-watch potential. Reuse the **structure**, not the substance.

## 1. Controlled comparison

Examples: cheap vs expensive, ten minutes vs ten hours, old method vs new method.

Retention engine: visible progression, contrast, escalating tests, final verdict.

Production leverage: one research template and consistent scoring framework.

Risk: superficial comparisons or predetermined conclusions.

## 2. Myth, misconception, or counterintuitive truth

Retention engine: viewer's current model conflicts with evidence.

Production leverage: research, diagrams, archival material, demonstrations.

Risk: manufacturing a false misconception or delaying the answer without new value.

## 3. How X became Y

Retention engine: transformation, turning points, decisions, consequences.

Production leverage: timeline, maps, charts, documents, voiceover.

Risk: chronological “and then” storytelling without causal stakes.

## 4. One mystery explained

Retention engine: a vivid unanswered question, clues, competing hypotheses, reveal.

Production leverage: focused research and custom visuals.

Risk: the answer is obvious, trivial, or buried beneath irrelevant history.

## 5. Ranked decisions with evidence

Retention engine: disagreement, criteria, escalating entries, surprising winner.

Production leverage: reusable scoring model.

Risk: generic lists, weak proof, or arbitrary ranking.

## 6. Experiment or simulation

Retention engine: prediction, method, setbacks, result, implication.

Production leverage: screen capture, data visualization, small physical tests.

Risk: unsafe experimentation or presenting simulation as reality.

## 7. Case-study breakdown

Retention engine: a high-stakes outcome and the hidden decisions behind it.

Production leverage: public records, screenshots, diagrams, narration.

Risk: hindsight bias or unsupported claims about intent.

## 8. Timeline under a constraint

Examples: 24 hours, 30 days, five stages, one budget.

Retention engine: progress bar, deadline, resource scarcity, escalating obstacles.

Production leverage: recurring visual UI and narration.

Risk: fake urgency or repetitive daily updates without story change.

## 9. Visualized data story

Retention engine: scale, pattern, anomaly, consequence.

Production leverage: charts, maps, motion templates, sourced data.

Risk: decorative charts, misleading axes, or no human stakes.

## 10. Search-led solution with browse framing

Retention engine: solve the user's problem while revealing a broader consequence or surprising best method.

Production leverage: screen recording, demonstration, before/after.

Risk: intrigue that obscures the practical answer.

## Format selection score

Rate 1-10:

- audience breadth;
- one-sentence clarity;
- curiosity tension;
- visual proof;
- progression/stakes;
- emotional variation;
- originality moat;
- monetization intent;
- production leverage;
- sequel potential.

Deduct for rights, safety, policy, evidence, and template-similarity risk.
