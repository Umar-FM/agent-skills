# 03 — Lead Generation Machine Skill

## Purpose

Use this skill to create predictable lead flow for a specific offer. The goal is not “more attention.” The goal is qualified conversations or actions that can become profitable customers.

Lead generation should begin only after the offer is clear enough to test. If the offer is weak, the lead plan should include an offer-repair step before scaling volume.

## Use this skill when the user asks for

- More leads.
- First clients.
- Lead magnets.
- Warm outreach.
- Cold outreach.
- Content plans.
- Paid ad tests.
- Referral systems.
- Affiliate systems.
- Agency or employee acquisition systems.
- A one-page advertising plan.
- “How do I get strangers to want to buy?”

## Required inputs

Ask for these if missing, or use labeled assumptions:

- Target buyer.
- Offer and price.
- Current lead channels.
- Monthly lead volume.
- Lead-to-call conversion.
- Show rate.
- Close rate.
- Gross profit per customer.
- Sales capacity.
- Time available for acquisition.
- Budget available for ads/tools/help.
- Existing audience, email list, network, customer base, or partners.

## Lead generation hierarchy

Use this hierarchy before choosing tactics:

1. **Offer clarity** — Can a prospect understand the outcome and why it matters?
2. **Audience specificity** — Can we find the exact people likely to care?
3. **Message-market match** — Does the message speak to a painful, current problem?
4. **Channel fit** — Does the target buyer actually pay attention there?
5. **Volume** — Are we doing enough repetitions to learn?
6. **Conversion** — Are replies, bookings, shows, closes, and economics working?
7. **Delegation/scale** — Can customers, employees, agencies, or affiliates help produce more lead flow?

## Core Four acquisition modes

Use four direct channels as the default acquisition options:

### 1. Warm outreach

Best for:

- First clients.
- New offers.
- Early validation.
- High-trust service businesses.
- Founder-led sales.

Examples:

- Past customers.
- Former colleagues.
- Friends of friends.
- Existing followers.
- Community members.
- Event contacts.
- LinkedIn connections.

### 2. Content

Best for:

- Building compounding trust.
- Educating a market.
- Demonstrating expertise.
- Creating inbound demand.
- Turning proof and lessons into distribution.

Examples:

- Short-form posts.
- Long-form videos.
- Newsletters.
- Case study breakdowns.
- Live workshops.
- Podcasts.
- Community posts.

### 3. Cold outreach

Best for:

- Specific B2B or high-value buyers.
- Clear pain and target list.
- Offers with strong direct relevance.
- Fast learning without ad spend.

Examples:

- Cold email.
- LinkedIn DMs.
- Instagram DMs.
- Phone.
- Direct mail.
- Loom audits.

### 4. Paid ads

Best for:

- Validated offers.
- Known audience/message.
- Trackable funnel.
- Sufficient gross profit and sales capacity.

Examples:

- Meta ads.
- Google search.
- YouTube.
- LinkedIn ads.
- TikTok.
- Retargeting.

## Expansion channels

After one direct channel works, expand through:

- Customers and referrals.
- Employees.
- Agencies.
- Affiliates.
- Partners.
- Communities.
- Events.
- Strategic content collaborations.

The sequence is important. Do not hire an agency or recruit affiliates before the offer and conversion process are clear enough for someone else to represent.

## Lead generation diagnosis

### If close rate is strong but calls are low

Likely bottleneck: lead volume.

Action:

- Increase warm outreach, content, cold outreach, paid test, or referrals.
- Do not rebuild the offer unless objections show a pattern.

### If leads respond but do not book

Likely bottleneck: call-to-action, lead magnet, or qualification.

Action:

- Improve CTA clarity.
- Reduce friction.
- Add proof.
- Make the next step less threatening.

### If calls book but do not show

Likely bottleneck: booking quality, reminder process, or urgency.

Action:

- Add confirmation, reminders, pre-call homework, calendar invites, and stronger reason to attend.

### If calls show but do not close

Likely bottleneck: offer, sales process, fit, proof, or price framing.

Action:

- Audit sales call recordings.
- Improve offer stack.
- Add proof/risk reversal.
- Tighten qualification.

### If customers buy but churn/refund

Likely bottleneck: fulfillment, expectation setting, onboarding, or customer fit.

Action:

- Fix onboarding and delivery before scaling leads.

## Workflow A — First 5 Clients Framework

Use for early-stage users, new offers, or new markets.

### Step 1 — Define the smallest credible offer

Create a clear paid offer that can be delivered manually.

Minimum:

- Specific buyer.
- Specific problem.
- Specific outcome.
- Specific delivery.
- Specific price.
- Specific next step.

### Step 2 — Build the warm list

Create a list of 100 people:

- Direct prospects.
- People who know prospects.
- Past customers.
- Former colleagues.
- Niche community members.
- Local or industry contacts.
- Friends who can refer.

### Step 3 — Send low-pressure direct messages

Message goal is not to close in the first message. The goal is to start a relevant conversation.

Warm outreach template:

> Hey [Name] — quick question. I’m testing a new [offer/category] for [specific buyer] who wants [outcome] without [pain/obstacle]. Do you know anyone dealing with [problem] right now? I’m looking for a few people to test it with and get feedback.

Direct prospect variation:

> Hey [Name] — noticed [specific context]. I’m helping [buyer type] solve [problem] and thought this might be relevant because [reason]. Open to me sending the short version?

### Step 4 — Offer a simple next step

Examples:

- 15-minute fit call.
- Free audit.
- Diagnostic scorecard.
- Loom teardown.
- Workshop.
- Paid pilot.

### Step 5 — Close pilot customers

For the first five, prioritize learning and proof:

- Charge enough that the buyer is committed.
- Deliver intensely.
- Collect proof.
- Capture objections.
- Improve the offer.

### Step 6 — Convert proof into acquisition assets

Create:

- Case study.
- Before/after snapshot.
- Testimonial.
- Objection FAQ.
- Content post.
- Ad angle.
- Referral ask.

## Workflow B — Lead Magnet Mastery

A lead magnet gives the buyer a reason to raise their hand before they are ready for a sales conversation.

### Good lead magnets

Good lead magnets are:

- Specific.
- Fast to consume.
- Directly connected to the paid offer.
- Useful enough to create trust.
- Diagnostic or implementation-oriented.
- Designed to attract buyers, not freebie seekers.

### Lead magnet formats

- Scorecard.
- Checklist.
- Calculator.
- Audit.
- Template.
- Swipe file.
- Mini-course.
- Workshop.
- Case study breakdown.
- Benchmark report.
- Diagnostic call.
- Quiz.
- Challenge.

### Lead magnet formula

> Help [specific buyer] get [small valuable outcome] or diagnose [painful problem] in [short time] without [major effort].

Examples:

- “The 12-Point Offer Audit for B2B Agencies.”
- “CAC Payback Calculator for Coaching Businesses.”
- “The 7-Day Local Gym Reactivation Checklist.”
- “LinkedIn Profile Teardown for Fractional CFOs.”

### Lead magnet QA

- Does it attract the same buyer as the paid offer?
- Does it reveal a problem the paid offer solves?
- Does it produce a fast win or clear diagnosis?
- Does it naturally lead to the next step?
- Can it be distributed in the chosen channel?
- Can it be fulfilled without heavy manual work?

## Workflow C — Content Method

Content should create trust and demand by repeatedly showing the market:

- The problem is real.
- The cost of inaction is high.
- The solution is possible.
- The creator understands the buyer.
- The creator has proof, taste, or expertise.
- The next step is obvious.

### Content pillars

1. Pain/problem education.
2. Mistakes and myths.
3. Frameworks and how-tos.
4. Proof and case studies.
5. Behind-the-scenes implementation.
6. Objection handling.
7. Contrarian beliefs.
8. Direct offers.

### Hook formulas

- “Most [buyer] think [common belief]. The real problem is [insight].”
- “If you’re [symptom], check [diagnostic].”
- “I reviewed [number] [assets/businesses]. The same problem appeared every time: [problem].”
- “Do this before you spend another dollar on [channel].”
- “The fastest way to improve [metric] is not [common tactic]. It’s [leverage point].”
- “Here are [number] reasons your [offer/funnel/sales process] is not converting.”

### Content-to-lead CTA options

- “Comment [keyword] and I’ll send the checklist.”
- “DM me [keyword] for the scorecard.”
- “Book the diagnostic if this is your bottleneck.”
- “Reply with your offer and I’ll score it.”
- “Grab the calculator/template in the link.”

### Content cadence

For early-stage founders:

- 1 proof or lesson post per day.
- 1 direct offer post per week.
- 10–20 meaningful comments per day in target communities.
- 10 direct conversations per day with engaged prospects.

For teams:

- Weekly pillar asset.
- Daily derivative posts.
- Weekly case study.
- Monthly workshop/webinar.
- Retargeting and nurture sequence.

## Workflow D — Cold Outreach Playbook

Cold outreach works when the list is specific, the problem is visible, and the message is relevant.

### Step 1 — Build the list

List quality beats volume.

List criteria:

- Buyer matches target profile.
- Problem is likely or visible.
- Buyer has ability to pay.
- Contact data is accurate.
- Message can be personalized.

### Step 2 — Create a reason to reach out

Reasons:

- Observed problem.
- Relevant trigger event.
- Industry benchmark.
- Competitor gap.
- Recent hiring/funding/product launch.
- Content they posted.
- Public review or customer feedback.
- Audit finding.

### Step 3 — Write the message

Cold message structure:

1. Specific context.
2. Problem/opportunity.
3. Credible mechanism or proof.
4. Low-friction CTA.

Template:

> Hey [Name] — noticed [specific observation]. A lot of [buyer type] with [context] run into [problem], which usually causes [cost]. I put together a quick [audit/checklist/example] showing [specific improvement]. Want me to send it over?

Follow-up 1:

> Worth sending, or not relevant right now?

Follow-up 2:

> No worries if not. The main thing I noticed was [one useful insight]. If you ever want the full teardown, happy to send it.

### Step 4 — Track the funnel

Track:

- Sent.
- Delivered.
- Opens if available.
- Replies.
- Positive replies.
- Calls booked.
- Shows.
- Sales.
- Revenue.
- Gross profit.
- Unsubscribes/complaints.

### Step 5 — Iterate one variable

Change one at a time:

- List segment.
- Trigger.
- First line.
- Problem statement.
- CTA.
- Offer.
- Proof.
- Follow-up cadence.

## Workflow E — Paid Ads Playbook

Use paid ads when the offer and funnel have some validation. Paid ads amplify economics; they do not fix a weak offer.

### Pre-ad checklist

- Clear offer.
- Defined avatar.
- Proof or strong reason to believe.
- Landing page or booking flow.
- Conversion tracking.
- Sales follow-up process.
- Gross profit can support CAC.
- Fulfillment can handle volume.

### Paid test structure

| Variable | Test plan |
|---|---|
| Audience | 1–3 clear segments |
| Creative | 3–5 angles |
| CTA | Lead magnet, call, webinar, purchase, diagnostic |
| Budget | Enough for directional data, not reckless scaling |
| Duration | 3–7 days initial read, longer if conversion cycle requires |
| Metrics | CTR, CPL, booked calls, show rate, CAC, gross profit, payback |
| Decision | Keep, kill, iterate |

### Ad angle types

- Pain agitation.
- Desired outcome.
- Contrarian belief.
- Case study.
- Mistake list.
- Diagnostic.
- Calculator/template.
- Before/after.
- Demonstration.
- Founder story.

### Paid ads kill criteria

Pause or change when:

- Lead quality is poor despite relevant targeting.
- Sales team cannot convert after enough calls.
- CAC exceeds gross profit tolerance.
- Funnel tracking is broken.
- Comments or compliance issues indicate misalignment.
- Fulfillment cannot support demand.

## Workflow F — Referral Playbook

Referrals work when customers have a clear reason and easy mechanism to introduce qualified prospects.

### Referral assets

- Referral script.
- Clear ideal prospect definition.
- One-sentence offer.
- Incentive or thank-you.
- Tracking method.
- Introduction template.
- Follow-up cadence.

### Customer referral ask

> Hey [Name], glad [specific result/progress]. Quick ask: do you know 1–2 [specific buyer] who are dealing with [problem] and would value [outcome]? If yes, I can send you a two-sentence intro blurb to make it easy.

### Referral QA

- Is the ideal referral specific?
- Is the ask low effort?
- Is the timing right after a win?
- Is there a clear next step?
- Is the incentive ethical and compliant?
- Are referred leads tracked separately?

## Workflow G — Affiliate Playbook

Affiliates work when there is a proven offer, clear attribution, acceptable margins, and brand-safe promotion.

### Affiliate setup

- Ideal affiliate profile.
- Commission structure.
- Attribution window.
- Payout terms.
- Approved claims.
- Prohibited claims.
- Creative assets.
- Tracking links.
- Lead quality rules.
- Termination rules.

### Affiliate risk controls

- No false scarcity.
- No unsupported income/health/performance claims.
- No spam.
- No bidding on prohibited keywords.
- No misrepresentation of affiliation.
- Clear review of creative and claims.

## Workflow H — Employees and Agencies

Delegating acquisition requires documented proof of what works.

### Hire/delegate only after

- Target buyer is clear.
- Offer has converted.
- Scripts/assets exist.
- Metrics are tracked.
- Quality standards are written.
- Feedback loop is weekly.

### Employee acquisition role scorecard

- Leads sourced.
- Qualified conversations.
- Booked calls.
- Show rate.
- Cost per booked call.
- Closed-won revenue.
- Quality/compliance issues.

### Agency scorecard

- Inputs: spend, creative volume, landing pages, tests run.
- Outputs: leads, booked calls, CAC, payback, gross profit.
- Quality: lead quality, brand safety, tracking accuracy, communication.

## Lead generation drills

### Drill 1 — 100 warm list

Build a list of 100 reachable people. Categorize as prospect, referral source, partner, past buyer, audience member.

### Drill 2 — 30 hooks

Write 30 hooks for one painful problem. Sort by specificity and urgency.

### Drill 3 — 10 lead magnets

Create 10 lead magnet ideas. Pick the one most connected to the paid offer.

### Drill 4 — 100 cold messages

Send 100 targeted messages in one week. Track response, positive response, booked calls, and objections.

### Drill 5 — Proof extraction

Interview three customers. Extract before state, after state, mechanism, objection, and quote-worthy moment.

### Drill 6 — Referral week

Ask 20 happy customers or contacts for one specific referral. Track introductions and close rate.

### Drill 7 — Paid ad mini-test

Create three ad angles, one lead magnet page, and a small test budget. Evaluate after enough data for directional learning.

## Lead machine QA checklist

Before scaling a lead channel:

- The offer has converted manually.
- The target buyer is specific.
- The message names a painful problem.
- The CTA is clear.
- Tracking is set up.
- Sales process can handle demand.
- Gross profit supports acquisition cost.
- Compliance risks are reviewed.
- Follow-up is ready.
- The test has a success threshold and kill condition.
