# YouTube Business Operating System

## Canonical states and owners

| State | Primary owner | Required artifact | Exit condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| `CHANNEL_THESIS` | Channel Strategy Architect | Channel thesis | Audience, promise, pillars, economics, constraints approved |
| `IDEA_POOL` | Idea Format Developer | Scored backlog | At least 20 viable ideas and 5 high-confidence candidates |
| `CONCEPT_GREENLIT` | Orchestrator | Concept brief | Idea score, proof path, production path, revenue fit pass |
| `PACKAGING_LOCKED` | Packaging Director | Packaging brief | Three distinct honest packages; one primary hypothesis |
| `RESEARCH_LOCKED` | Research Fact Checker | Research packet | Claims sourced; gaps and uncertainty labeled; rights plan present |
| `SCRIPT_LOCKED` | Story Retention Scriptwriter | Script + retention map | Promise match, causal progression, proof, payoff, runtime pass |
| `ASSETS_READY` | Faceless Visual Producer | Asset manifest | Every beat has a visual plan and rights status |
| `ROUGH_CUT` | Retention Video Editor | Rough-cut review | Story works without polish; major confusion removed |
| `FINAL_CUT` | Editor + Audio + Compliance | Final review | Clarity, mix, pace, rights, disclosure, sponsor integration pass |
| `PUBLISH_READY` | Publishing Manager | Upload packet | Metadata, tests, end screen, chapters, disclosures, QA complete |
| `LIVE` | Publishing Manager | Live URL / record | Video public and monitored |
| `POSTMORTEM` | Analytics Scientist | Postmortem | Primary bottleneck diagnosed with evidence |
| `ITERATION` | Orchestrator | Decision log | Scale, revise, repackage, sequel, archive, or stop decision |

## The stage-gate protocol

Every gate is binary: **PASS** or **RETURN WITH REVISIONS**. Agents may attach uncertainty, but they may not silently lower the standard. The orchestrator records:

- decision;
- owner;
- evidence;
- unresolved risks;
- revision request;
- due state, not due date.

## Role routing

### Strategy lane

`channel-strategy-architect -> audience-market-researcher -> finance-unit-economics -> monetization-revenue-architect`

### Idea lane

`trend-competitive-intelligence -> idea-format-developer -> packaging-director`

### Creation lane

`research-fact-checker -> story-retention-scriptwriter -> faceless-visual-producer -> voice-sound-producer -> retention-video-editor`

### Release lane

`rights-policy-brand-safety -> publishing-seo-distribution -> community-audience-development -> shorts-repurposing-strategist`

### Learning and revenue lane

`analytics-experimentation-scientist -> monetization-revenue-architect -> sponsorship-affiliate-sales -> finance-unit-economics`

`production-operations-manager` supports all lanes; `youtube-business-orchestrator` owns final prioritization.

## Weekly operating rhythm

### Portfolio review

- Review channel and business scorecard.
- Compare actual results against pre-publish predictions.
- Decide the mix of proven formats, adjacent bets, and experiments.
- Protect production capacity for the highest expected contribution, not the loudest request.

### Greenlight meeting

- Review top concepts and packages together.
- Reject concepts that only work after a long explanation.
- Verify proof, access, rights, differentiation, and monetization.
- Assign a primary learning hypothesis.

### Production review

- Inspect research packet, opening, retention map, asset plan, and cost.
- Cut scope before cutting quality.
- Freeze changes after script lock unless a new fact, rights issue, or superior story requires reopening.

### Postmortem meeting

- Separate audience reach from audience response.
- Diagnose one primary and up to two secondary bottlenecks.
- Convert findings into changes to templates, rubrics, or future experiments.

## Portfolio allocation

A default allocation for an established lane:

- **70% proven audience-format fit**
- **20% adjacent expansion**
- **10% high-variance experiments**

For a new channel, increase experiments but maintain thematic coherence. Paddy Galloway's audience-overlap framing is useful: most uploads should plausibly interest a large majority of the existing audience, with a controlled minority used to explore new territory.

## Escalation rules

Escalate to the orchestrator when:

- two agents disagree on the core viewer or promise;
- the best package exaggerates beyond the video's proof;
- a factual claim materially affects safety, finance, law, health, politics, or reputation;
- production cost exceeds the approved range by more than 15%;
- sponsor requirements threaten the opening or editorial conclusion;
- rights cannot be documented;
- the concept cannot be made materially distinct from recent uploads;
- analytics support more than one plausible diagnosis.
