# Purpose

Turn attention into a resilient business. Match revenue mechanisms to audience intent and channel maturity, sequence them responsibly, model their effect on viewing, and avoid dependence on a single volatile stream.

## Use this skill when

- Creating the channel business model.
- Approaching or entering YPP.
- Adding sponsors, affiliates, products, memberships, or licensing.
- RPM is weak, revenue is concentrated, or monetization harms retention.

## Required inputs

- Channel thesis, audience intent, geography, and advertiser suitability.
- Current eligibility, revenue analytics, and traffic mix.
- Product/sponsor categories, affiliate programs, and owned capabilities.
- Production cost and cash requirements.
- Trust, legal, and disclosure constraints.

## Non-negotiable operating rules

- Verify current YouTube eligibility, feature availability, and monetization policies before action.
- Monetize the reason viewers came; avoid unrelated high-commission offers.
- Deliver meaningful value before interruptive promotion wherever practical.
- Model gross revenue, net contribution, cash timing, and retention impact separately.
- Diversify only after each layer has a credible audience fit and operator.

# Workflow

1. Map viewer intent by content pillar: entertainment, learning, problem-solving, comparison, purchase research, identity, or community.
2. Design a maturity ladder: pre-YPP audience proof; ads/Premium; context-fit affiliates; direct sponsors; owned products/services; memberships/community; licensing or commerce where appropriate.
3. For each stream, define eligibility, audience fit, expected value, operational burden, disclosure, risk, and attribution.
4. Identify monetizable moments in the viewer journey without distorting the concept or opening.
5. Model scenarios using views, RPM/effective RPM, conversion, commission, refund, fulfillment, and variable cost ranges.
6. Coordinate sponsor and affiliate placements with the script/editor to protect the first meaningful payoff and maintain relevance.
7. Build owned audience assets such as a newsletter only when they extend the channel promise and provide clear value.
8. Track revenue by video, pillar, source, geography, and time horizon.
9. Review concentration, seasonality, advertiser suitability, and dependence on platform features.
10. Recommend tests ranked by expected contribution, learning, trust risk, and operational effort.

## Decision rules

- AdSense is a layer, not the whole business.
- High affiliate conversion on low-trust content is not a durable win.
- Products should solve an audience problem repeatedly visible in content and feedback.
- Memberships require recurring identity/community value; they are weak fits for purely transactional utility channels.
- A sponsor that demands editorial distortion, misleading claims, or excessive rights should be declined.

# Required outputs

- Monetization ladder by channel stage.
- Revenue architecture by pillar and viewer intent.
- Placement and attribution rules.
- Scenario model and concentration dashboard.
- Quarterly monetization experiment roadmap.

# Quality gate

PASS only when:

- Every revenue stream has a clear audience-value rationale.
- Current eligibility and disclosure requirements are verified.
- Net contribution and operational burden are modeled.
- Promotion placement protects the video’s opening and payoff.
- No single stream creates unacceptable concentration or trust risk.

# Handoff

Send sponsor opportunities to `sponsorship-affiliate-sales`, product/community opportunities to the orchestrator and community role, configuration to `publishing-seo-distribution`, and models to `finance-unit-economics`.

## Metrics owned

- Revenue per 1,000 views and impressions.
- Revenue per watch hour and production hour.
- Contribution margin by stream and pillar.
- Revenue concentration and seasonality.
- Retention impact around monetization segments.
- Affiliate/product conversion and refund/complaint rate.

## Failure modes to prevent

- Choosing topics solely for CPM.
- Adding many low-fit affiliate links.
- Treating sponsor gross fee as profit.
- Building products before repeated audience demand exists.
- Ignoring taxes, refunds, commissions, fulfillment, and payment timing.
