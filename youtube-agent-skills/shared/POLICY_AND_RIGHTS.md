# Policy, Rights, AI, and Brand Safety

## Current baseline checked 2026-06-23

Always verify current official documentation before publishing.

### Monetization originality

YouTube's monetization policies require original and authentic content. The platform's current language identifies **inauthentic content** as mass-produced or repetitive material, including generic AI templates that create the impression of mass production without original perspective. Reused material requires significant commentary, substantive modification, or educational/entertainment value. Permission alone does not guarantee monetization eligibility.

### AI disclosure

Disclose realistic content that has been meaningfully altered or synthetically generated when required by YouTube's current rules. Maintain an internal AI-use log even when disclosure is not required. Never imitate a real person's voice or likeness without authorization and appropriate disclosure.

### Copyright and licensing

For every external asset, document one basis:

- original creation;
- written license;
- stock subscription covering the intended use;
- public-domain status;
- Creative Commons terms satisfied;
- platform remix license;
- a fact-specific copyright exception reviewed by qualified counsel where stakes justify it.

“Fair use” is not a magic phrase, disclaimer, or guaranteed platform defense. Use the minimum necessary material, add genuine transformation, and preserve source records.

### Paid promotions and affiliates

Use YouTube's paid-promotion disclosure when applicable and comply with the laws of the target audience. Disclose affiliate relationships clearly and near the recommendation. Do not make claims the sponsor cannot substantiate.

### Advertiser suitability

Assess topic, language, graphic material, dangerous acts, sensitive events, and thumbnails before production. Context can matter, but revenue risk must be modeled rather than discovered after publishing.

## Mandatory rights ledger

Each asset row must contain:

- asset ID;
- exact source URL or file origin;
- creator/rightsholder;
- license or legal basis;
- proof file or receipt;
- permitted platforms and territories;
- expiration or subscription status;
- modifications made;
- credit requirement;
- final disposition.

## High-risk content escalation

Escalate medical, legal, financial, political, election, safety, crime, accusations, minors, private individuals, graphic events, or reputational allegations to a qualified reviewer. The fact-checker must distinguish public record, allegation, dispute, and verified conclusion.

## Faceless risk checklist

Reject or redesign when:

- the script is primarily a reading of another article or feed;
- footage is a compilation with minimal commentary;
- many videos use the same template with only names/numbers swapped;
- the narrator implies firsthand experience that did not occur;
- synthetic imagery could be mistaken for documentary evidence;
- the channel cannot prove how it participated in creating the content;
- a sponsor dictates misleading editorial claims;
- rights depend on a subscription that does not cover client, team, ad, or broadcast use.
