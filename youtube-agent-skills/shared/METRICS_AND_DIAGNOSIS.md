# Metrics and Diagnosis

## Metric hierarchy

### Viewer acquisition

- Impressions by surface
- Impressions CTR by surface
- Views from impressions
- Search terms and Suggested-video neighbors
- New versus returning viewers

### Viewing quality

- Watch time
- Average view duration (AVD)
- Average percentage viewed (APV)
- Percentage still watching at 30 seconds
- Relative retention versus similar-length videos
- Dips, spikes, top moments, and rewatch behavior
- End-screen element click rate

### Satisfaction and audience fit

- Likes/dislikes where available
- Shares
- Comments that demonstrate delivered value
- Subscribers gained per qualified view
- Returning viewers
- Follow-on video consumption and playlist movement
- Survey/satisfaction signals are not fully visible; use proxies cautiously

### Business quality

- RPM and revenue by source
- Sponsor effective RPM
- Affiliate earnings per 1,000 views
- Revenue per 1,000 impressions
- Revenue per production hour
- Contribution margin per video
- Cash payback period
- Evergreen revenue after 28/90 days

## Derived creator-side metrics

### Qualified watch time per impression

`QWTPI minutes = CTR as decimal x AVD minutes`

Example: 6% CTR and 6-minute AVD gives `0.06 x 6 = 0.36 minutes` watched per registered impression.

Use this only to compare similar audiences and surfaces. It omits uncounted impressions, satisfaction, session effects, and personalization.

### Revenue per 1,000 impressions

`Revenue / registered impressions x 1,000`

Useful for comparing packaging + viewing + monetization as one business outcome.

### Contribution margin

`Total attributable revenue - variable production cost - commissions - traffic acquisition cost`

Exclude sunk overhead when deciding whether to repeat a format; include full overhead in company planning.

## Diagnosis matrix

| Pattern | Most likely bottleneck | Check next | Typical response |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low impressions, strong CTR and viewing | Audience size, competition, or limited test | Surface mix, topic breadth, viewer overlap | Broaden concept or package; create an adjacent gateway video |
| Strong impressions, weak CTR | Idea framing or package | Home/Suggested CTR, one-second clarity | Repackage with a different hypothesis |
| Strong CTR, weak 30-second retention | Promise mismatch or slow confirmation | First 5/15/30 seconds | Rewrite opening; align package; remove setup |
| Strong intro, steady mid-video decline | Weak progression or monotony | Beat density, stakes, visual proof | Add meaningful progress, complications, emotional variation |
| Sharp dip at sponsor | Integration timing or relevance | Sponsor start/end and message length | Move after first payoff; rewrite around viewer benefit |
| Spikes on explanation | Valuable insight or confusion | Comments, rewatch context | Expand if valued; clarify if confusing |
| High AVD, low APV | Long video with engaged core | Similar-length comparison | Do not shorten automatically; inspect absolute watch value |
| High APV, low AVD | Video may be too short for its idea | Session movement, viewer demand | Consider deeper sequel rather than padding current video |
| Views high, returning viewers weak | One-off topic or off-promise hit | Audience overlap and next-video CTR | Build a bridge video or avoid chasing unrelated virality |
| RPM strong, contribution negative | Production economics | Cost by stage, sponsor/affiliate fit | Reduce scope, reuse systems, improve revenue stack |
| Search works, browse stalls | Utility is clear; broad intrigue is weak | Browse package and emotional framing | Translate narrow intent into broader stakes or comparisons |
| Browse works, search stalls | Curiosity works; explicit intent is weak | Search terms and evergreen demand | Create a separate searchable companion video |

## Measurement rules

- Never use one universal CTR target. YouTube notes that CTR varies by content, audience, and surface; broad Home distribution usually lowers CTR.
- Compare videos of similar length, traffic source, audience maturity, and age.
- Do not react to tiny early samples.
- Use native concurrent A/B tests where eligible; YouTube determines winners by watch-time share.
- Record title/thumbnail changes and timing so before/after analysis remains interpretable.
- Paid traffic must be separated from organic viewing.
- Shorts use different acquisition metrics: viewed versus swiped away, engaged views, completion, rewatches, AVD, and APV.

## Postmortem questions

1. Who actually watched, and from where?
2. Did the intended viewer click at a competitive rate on the intended surface?
3. Did the opening prove the click was correct?
4. Where did the video create or lose momentum?
5. Did the promised payoff arrive and feel proportionate?
6. Did viewers want another video from this channel?
7. Was the revenue congruent with the audience's intent?
8. What single change has the highest expected impact next time?
