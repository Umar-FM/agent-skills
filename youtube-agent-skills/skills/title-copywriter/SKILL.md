# Purpose

Express the most compelling truthful framing in the fewest useful words. Create title options that communicate subject, tension, and payoff while leaving the thumbnail a distinct job.

## Use this skill when

- A packaging director needs title routes.
- A search-led or browse-led video is being prepared.
- An evergreen video needs repackaging.
- Titles are verbose, generic, repetitive, or disconnected from the actual payoff.

## Required inputs

- Concept and packaging briefs.
- Target viewer and traffic surface.
- Thumbnail concepts and information they already communicate.
- Audience language bank, search intent, and competitor audit.
- Exact result, stakes, and proof available in the video.

## Non-negotiable operating rules

- Accuracy is mandatory; curiosity comes from framing, not deception.
- Front-load the most important words because titles truncate.
- Write separately for browse and search before choosing a hybrid.
- Use concrete nouns, active verbs, meaningful contrast, and specific stakes.
- Avoid filler, unexplained episode labels, excessive caps, emoji, and redundant thumbnail text.

# Workflow

1. Extract the title ingredients: subject, action, transformation, conflict, constraint, result, scale, time, and viewer consequence.
2. Choose the job: searchable clarity, browse intrigue, subscriber familiarity, or a deliberate combination.
3. Generate at least 30 raw titles across frameworks: direct result, question, anomaly, versus, transformation, constraint, investigation, ranking, and counterintuitive claim.
4. Remove titles that depend on hype words without a specific idea.
5. Pair each finalist with the thumbnail and identify what each medium contributes.
6. Read titles aloud and test for ambiguity, pronoun confusion, jargon, and mobile truncation.
7. Verify every implied fact, superlative, timeline, and outcome against the research and final cut.
8. Select three titles representing distinct hypotheses. Do not make variants differ by only one adjective.
9. Record expected audience interpretation and opening obligation for each title.
10. After publication, preserve the title history and analyze changes in context.

## Decision rules

- Search titles should include the intent phrase naturally and clearly; browse titles should make a cold viewer care without prior intent.
- Numbers are useful when they represent meaningful scale, comparison, or constraint, not decoration.
- Questions work when the answer is non-obvious and worth the runtime.
- Do not reveal the entire payoff when the journey supplies value, but do not conceal the basic subject.
- If the strongest title is misleading, the concept needs redesign rather than euphemism.

# Required outputs

- Title bank grouped by hypothesis and surface.
- Three finalists paired with thumbnails.
- Accuracy and truncation check.
- Opening obligations created by each title.
- Change log for post-publish tests.

# Quality gate

PASS only when:

- Finalists are short enough to retain the important words on common screens.
- Each title is clear, specific, accurate, and emotionally legible.
- Each pair complements its thumbnail.
- The three options test genuinely different frames.
- No claim exceeds the evidence in the final cut.

# Handoff

Return finalists to `packaging-director`; send factual wording to `research-fact-checker`; send the locked promise to `story-retention-scriptwriter`; send final variants to `publishing-seo-distribution`.

## Metrics owned

- CTR and watch-time share by title hypothesis.
- Search query match and search watch time.
- 30-second retention after title changes.
- Rate of misleading-interpretation feedback.
- Performance of title frameworks by pillar.

## Failure modes to prevent

- Writing “You Won’t Believe” titles that hide the subject.
- Stuffing search keywords into unnatural language.
- Repeating thumbnail text.
- Making tiny wording tests that cannot teach anything.
- Changing a title without logging timing and hypothesis.
