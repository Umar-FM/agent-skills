# Purpose

Release each video accurately and route the right viewers into and through the channel. Treat metadata and distribution as audience matching and session design, not keyword superstition.

## Use this skill when

- A final cut and package are approved.
- A search-led evergreen video needs intent alignment.
- A video requires chapters, playlists, end screens, disclosures, or external launch assets.
- An older library needs metadata or packaging maintenance.

## Required inputs

- Final master and approved title/thumbnail variants.
- Upload packet template, captions, chapters, source/credit requirements, and disclosures.
- Target surface, search intent, playlist/series map, and next-video destination.
- Monetization and sponsor settings.
- Launch timing and distribution channels.

## Non-negotiable operating rules

- Title and thumbnail are the primary packaging; tags have limited value except cases such as common misspellings.
- Metadata must accurately reflect the final cut.
- Use chapters to improve navigation and context, not to excuse poor pacing.
- Route viewers to the single best next video rather than offering too many choices.
- Track paid and external traffic separately from organic performance.

# Workflow

1. Verify the final file, aspect ratio, duration, audio, captions, and thumbnail exports.
2. Lock the primary title-thumbnail pair and configure up to three native A/B hypotheses where eligible.
3. Write the description for human clarity: opening value summary, relevant links/disclosures, sources or credits, related playlist, and only useful keywords.
4. Create accurate chapters for longer videos with descriptive labels.
5. Choose playlist/series placement and design the end screen around the strongest next-viewer action.
6. Add cards only where they support the current viewing journey and do not distract from a key moment.
7. Set audience, age, language, category, monetization, ad, paid-promotion, AI/alteration, and other current disclosure settings.
8. Upload and inspect YouTube’s processing, copyright checks, caption synchronization, mobile thumbnail, chapters, and end-screen safe area.
9. Prepare pinned comment and distribution copy tailored to community posts, newsletter, site, and social platforms. Do not duplicate the title without adding a reason to watch.
10. Schedule release based on operational readiness and audience context, not myths about a universal best time.
11. Log the publication configuration and begin the analytics monitoring plan.

## Decision rules

- Use searchable titles for explicit intent and intriguing titles for browse; the packaging director chooses the primary job.
- External distribution is useful when it reaches audience-fit viewers; large mismatched bursts can distort interpretation, so segment the data.
- Do not change title or thumbnail impulsively while a native test is running.
- An end screen should normally offer one primary next video plus subscribe only when the layout remains clear.
- Old evergreen metadata can be refreshed when the content remains accurate and history is logged.

# Required outputs

- Completed `shared/templates/upload_packet.md`.
- Published video record and variant configuration.
- Description, chapters, captions, playlist, cards, end screen, pinned comment, and distribution copy.
- Launch QA checklist and issue log.
- Metadata/change history.

# Quality gate

PASS only when:

- The public page accurately represents the final cut and required disclosures.
- A/B variants are distinct and correctly configured.
- Captions, chapters, links, credits, and end screens work on mobile and desktop.
- The video routes to a deliberate next-viewing experience.
- Launch data can be interpreted because traffic and changes are logged.

# Handoff

Send the live record to `analytics-experimentation-scientist`, community assets to `community-audience-development`, short-form opportunities to `shorts-repurposing-strategist`, and monetization configuration to revenue roles.

## Metrics owned

- Home/Suggested/Search CTR by launch configuration.
- End-screen and playlist movement.
- Caption and chapter usage where available.
- External-to-organic audience quality.
- Publishing error and correction rate.

## Failure modes to prevent

- Spending hours on tags while neglecting package or next-video routing.
- Publishing with broken links, captions, or disclosures.
- Changing multiple variables without logging them.
- Using generic distribution copy that attracts low-fit clicks.
- Treating metadata as a substitute for audience demand.
