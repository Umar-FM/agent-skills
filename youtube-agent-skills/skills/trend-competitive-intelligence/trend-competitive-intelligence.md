# Purpose

Continuously scan the market for changes in viewer appetite, packaging language, production economics, and repeatable formats. Explain why an outlier likely worked, what is transferable, what is already saturated, and whether the opportunity fits the channel.

## Use this skill when

- Building the idea backlog or quarterly strategy.
- A format is declining or competitors are copying it.
- The channel needs adjacent growth opportunities.
- A breaking trend may create a time-sensitive video.

## Required inputs

- Channel thesis and content pillars.
- Peer, aspirational, substitute, and cross-niche channel sets.
- Time window and target region/language.
- Available analytics tools and public data.
- Production and policy constraints.

## Non-negotiable operating rules

- Use current web and platform data; record observation dates.
- Normalize performance by channel size, upload age, format, and typical baseline where possible.
- Prioritize recent low-subscriber or young-channel outliers when searching for early demand signals.
- Study underperformers as well as winners to avoid survivorship bias.
- Transfer structures and viewer tensions, not copyrighted assets or another creator’s identity.
- A trend is not strategy unless it connects to the channel promise.

# Workflow

1. Create four channel sets: direct peers, audience substitutes, world-class packaging references, and unrelated niches with transferable formats.
2. Collect recent uploads, titles, thumbnails, view velocity, length, topic, format, and apparent target surface.
3. Identify true outliers relative to each channel's baseline rather than raw view count. Record recent high-view/low-subscriber anomalies separately.
4. Decompose each outlier into idea, familiarity, unexpected twist, title formula, thumbnail formula, first-30-second promise, access/proof, retention engine, emotional engine, and timing.
5. Compare with non-outliers to identify the differentiating variable rather than assuming every visible feature caused success.
6. Track repeated packaging patterns and estimate saturation: emerging, accelerating, mature, copied, or declining.
7. Search cross-niche formats that the target audience has likely not seen, such as tier comparisons, timed tests, reversals, simulations, or investigations.
8. Estimate trend half-life and production lead time. Reject ideas that will arrive after the audience moves on unless the topic has evergreen value.
9. Assess rights, advertiser, reputational, and misinformation risks.
10. Produce opportunity briefs with transferable principle, adaptation idea, original value-add, evidence, confidence, and kill criteria.

## Decision rules

- Favor structural novelty: a familiar promise delivered through an unexpected comparison, constraint, proof source, or visual frame.
- Do not call an upload an outlier until it materially exceeds the creator’s relevant baseline.
- A crowded format can still work if the audience remains responsive; evolve proof and perspective before abandoning it.
- Breaking news requires faster verification and higher source discipline, not lower standards.
- If an opportunity depends on privileged access the channel lacks, redesign around a proof advantage it owns.

# Required outputs

- Weekly or monthly competitive scan.
- Outlier database with normalized context.
- Format-transfer library.
- Comment-gap and first-30-second pattern notes where available.
- Trend opportunity briefs and saturation map.
- Competitor package and positioning gaps.

# Quality gate

PASS only when:

- Every claimed trend has multiple observations or is labeled speculative.
- Outliers are compared with relevant baselines.
- Transfer recommendations explain why the structure fits this channel.
- No recommendation depends on copying protected assets, deception, or inaccessible proof.
- Time-sensitive opportunities include a realistic publish-by threshold.

# Handoff

Send opportunity briefs to `idea-format-developer` and strategic shifts to `channel-strategy-architect`. Send packaging patterns to `packaging-director`; send saturation and timing risks to the orchestrator.

## Metrics owned

- Percentage of trend briefs that become viable concepts.
- Outlier hit rate relative to control ideas.
- Lead time from signal detection to publish.
- Performance of cross-niche format transfers.
- False-positive rate and postmortem accuracy.

## Failure modes to prevent

- Sorting by raw views and calling the top videos “research.”
- Copying a title/thumbnail without understanding the viewer tension.
- Chasing trends after the half-life expires.
- Assuming correlation proves why a video worked.
- Letting competitive research erase the channel’s own voice.
