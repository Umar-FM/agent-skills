# Purpose

Turn isolated views into a recognizable audience relationship. Listen systematically, reinforce the channel promise, create reasons to return, and feed high-signal language and questions back into content development.

## Use this skill when

- Returning viewers or next-video movement are weak.
- The channel needs audience feedback, community posts, or recurring rituals.
- A series, membership, live element, or newsletter is being considered.
- Comments reveal confusion, demand, or trust issues.

## Required inputs

- Channel thesis and brand voice.
- Recent uploads, comments, audience analytics, community tools, and moderation policies.
- Upcoming slate and next-video bridges.
- Monetization/community plans.
- Safety and privacy constraints.

## Non-negotiable operating rules

- Community work serves belonging, feedback, and future viewing; it is not engagement bait.
- Respond in the channel’s voice and never imply a personal relationship the team cannot maintain.
- Protect private information and moderate harassment, scams, and unsafe behavior.
- Treat comments as qualitative evidence, not representative polling.
- Close the loop when audience input changes a video or correction.

# Workflow

1. Define the desired relationship: informed peer, curious club, challenge participants, industry insiders, learners, or another identity aligned with the thesis.
2. Create a moderation and response guide covering tone, corrections, criticism, spoilers, scams, harassment, and escalation.
3. Review comments by theme: delight, confusion, disagreement, requests, objections, quotes, purchase intent, and next-video questions.
4. Respond early to high-value comments, correct material errors transparently, and surface useful context through pinned comments.
5. Use community posts to test language, choices, predictions, and interest without pretending informal polls are statistically valid.
6. Build recurring rituals: progress updates, series scoreboards, viewer submissions, corrections, behind-the-scenes evidence, or next-episode decisions.
7. Design next-video bridges in coordination with scripts, end screens, playlists, and pinned comments.
8. Feed audience language and unresolved questions into research, titles, ideas, and product/sponsor decisions.
9. Track returning viewers, repeat commenters, community-post response, and movement into related videos.
10. Escalate safety, legal, privacy, or reputational issues promptly.

## Decision rules

- Do not ask questions merely to inflate comments; ask when answers can shape content or deepen the experience.
- High-comment controversy can be strategically harmful if trust and advertiser fit decline.
- A community product or membership requires recurring value beyond access to the creator’s identity.
- Viewer requests should be weighted by channel fit, repeated demand, and silent-audience behavior.
- Use corrections as a trust asset, not something to hide.

# Required outputs

- Community voice and moderation guide.
- Weekly comment insight report.
- Community-post and ritual calendar.
- Next-video bridge plan.
- Audience questions, language, and correction log.

# Quality gate

PASS only when:

- Community activity reinforces the channel promise.
- High-signal feedback is categorized and routed.
- Safety, scam, privacy, and moderation processes are clear.
- Audience participation has a genuine purpose.
- Corrections and material responses are transparent.

# Handoff

Send insights to audience, idea, title, script, monetization, and analytics roles. Send safety/reputation issues to `rights-policy-brand-safety`; send next-video routing to publishing.

## Metrics owned

- Returning viewers.
- Next-video and playlist movement.
- Comment quality and recurring participation.
- Community-post response and idea validation rate.
- Correction response time and trust indicators.

## Failure modes to prevent

- Confusing comment volume with audience health.
- Letting the loudest viewers dictate the whole channel.
- Using manipulative engagement bait.
- Ignoring scams and impersonation.
- Building a community around the operator rather than the channel promise when the brand is faceless.
