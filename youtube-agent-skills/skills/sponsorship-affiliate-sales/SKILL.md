# Purpose

Build a repeatable commercial pipeline without turning the channel into an ad inventory machine. Sell the audience-value context, protect editorial control, and structure integrations that viewers find relevant and useful.

## Use this skill when

- The channel has a defined audience and repeatable viewership.
- A sponsor, agency, affiliate program, or brand approaches.
- A campaign needs pricing, negotiation, integration, tracking, or reporting.
- Commercial segments show retention damage or weak conversion.

## Required inputs

- Audience/channel media kit and recent performance distribution.
- Target brand categories and audience problem map.
- Video slate and integration opportunities.
- Rate floor, inventory, exclusivity limits, usage-rights policy, and legal terms.
- Disclosure and advertiser-suitability requirements.

## Non-negotiable operating rules

- Qualify brand-audience fit before discussing price.
- Protect editorial conclusion and factual accuracy.
- Disclose sponsorships and affiliates clearly under platform and applicable legal rules.
- Do not guarantee views, conversions, or outcomes outside controlled deliverables.
- Price usage rights, exclusivity, revisions, rush work, and cross-platform assets separately.

# Workflow

1. Build a prospect list from products the audience already uses, asks about, compares, or needs. Prioritize direct problem fit.
2. Create a concise pitch showing audience promise, relevant examples, expected integration concept, and business outcome without inflated claims.
3. Qualify budget, objectives, timeline, deliverables, approval process, claim substantiation, target geography, and measurement.
4. Price using expected reach, audience value, integration depth, production cost, opportunity cost, exclusivity, usage, and payment risk. Maintain a walk-away floor.
5. Negotiate a deal memo: fee, deliverables, placement, talking points, substantiated claims, disclosure, revision limits, cancellation, make-goods, usage, exclusivity, payment schedule, and reporting.
6. Develop the integration after the video concept is approved. Connect the product to a genuine viewer need and place it after an initial payoff where feasible.
7. Have research and compliance verify claims, comparisons, privacy, and required disclosures.
8. Track links, codes, landing pages, conversion windows, and platform metrics without overstating attribution.
9. Report delivery and outcomes honestly; record brand quality, payment behavior, viewer response, and renewal potential.
10. Maintain a category/exclusivity calendar and avoid sponsor saturation.

## Decision rules

- Decline brands that conflict with the channel promise, advertiser safety, audience welfare, or factual standards.
- Use flat fee, performance, or hybrid structures based on controllability and downside risk.
- A lower-paying high-fit sponsor can outperform a high-fee mismatch through retention, conversion, and renewal.
- Do not accept perpetual broad usage rights at an ordinary integration price.
- Make-goods should address missed contracted deliverables, not uncontrollable performance guarantees.

# Required outputs

- Prospect pipeline and outreach assets.
- Completed `shared/templates/sponsor_brief.md`.
- Deal memo and approval log.
- Integration script, disclosure, and tracking plan.
- Campaign report and renewal recommendation.

# Quality gate

PASS only when:

- The brand solves a real audience need and fits the video.
- All claims are substantiated and disclosures are clear.
- Commercial terms protect editorial control, payment, usage, and revision scope.
- Integration timing and length are reviewed for retention risk.
- Tracking and reporting limitations are explicit.

# Handoff

Send integration requirements to script/editor/publishing roles, claims to `research-fact-checker`, legal/policy issues to `rights-policy-brand-safety`, and fee/cost data to `finance-unit-economics` and monetization.

## Metrics owned

- Qualified pipeline, close rate, and renewal rate.
- Sponsor effective RPM and net contribution.
- Retention change across integration.
- Affiliate conversion and earnings per 1,000 views.
- Payment days, revision burden, and viewer complaint rate.

## Failure modes to prevent

- Selling every available upload.
- Accepting vague brand talking points without substantiation.
- Hiding the disclosure or making the ad indistinguishable from editorial claims.
- Underpricing exclusivity and usage rights.
- Reporting correlation as conversion causation.
